class="top"><h1>Avoiding Heartburn When You're on the Go</h1><h2>The average fast-food menu isn't very concerned with helping you avoid heartburn, but there are ways to eat on the go, and quickly, without inviting consequences.</h2></div><div class="bottom"><div class="floatl"><div class="byline">By <a href=/contributing-writers-and-editors.aspx>Beth W. Orenstein</a></div><div class="reviewedby">Medically reviewed by <a href=/medical-reviewers.aspx>Lindsey Marcellin, MD, MPH</a></div></div><div class="printbox"><img class="print" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/icon_print_16px.gif" alt="Print Article"><a href="javascript: _gotoprintcs('28AC47E1-024D-41A4-8CD4-2B238BC4CD17');">Print</a><img id="ctl01_ctl03_imgEmail" class="email" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/icon_email_16px.gif" alt="E-mail Article"><a class="email" href="javascript: _sendtofriend(&quot;Avoiding Heartburn When You're on the Go&quot;);">E-mail</a></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div id="article" xmlns:fb="http://www.facebook.com/2008/fbml"><div class="article"><p>You&#8217;re on the road, you're in a hurry, and you&#8217;re hungry. A fast-food restaurant probably seems pretty appealing. The problem is, practically everything on most fast-food menus is, or at least includes, a heartburn-causing food — tomatoes, onions, ketchup, fried foods, chocolate ice cream, carbonated beverages, and caffeine. </p><div><img src="&#xD;&#xA;            http://images.agoramedia.com/cs/eh/cs_heartburn_fast_food_article.jpg" alt="Heartburn on the go"></div><p>We tend to indulge in <a href="/health-report/understanding-heartburn.aspx">heartburn</a> trigger foods at fast-food restaurants, and we &#8220;also tend to eat bigger portions,&#8221; says Sanjay Agrawal, MD, a gastroenterologist who practices in Gig Harbor, Wash. And overeating, it should go without saying, is another common <a href="/health-report/heartburn/heartburn-causes-and-triggers.aspx">cause of heartburn</a>.</p></p><p>What can you do? Here are some tips for eating on the run without risking heartburn:</p><br /> &#160;<br /><ul><li><strong>Order a kid&#8217;s meal.</strong> At most fast-food restaurants, anybody can order a kid's meal. The kid&#8217;s meal is usually smaller, which means you'll be less likely to overeat. Some people can even get away with consuming small amounts of their <a href="/heartburn-photos/cooking-tips-for-heartburn.aspx">heartburn trigger foods</a>. Besides, Dr. Agrawal says, &#8220;a kid&#8217;s meal is enough food for many people.&#8221; You can also check to see whether the restaurant offers specials for senior citizens, he adds, which are smaller in size and less spicy.</li><li><strong>Stick to the regular size.</strong> Avoiding heartburn requires portion control. Although super-sizing your meal may seem like a bargain, consider whether you need the extra food — eating it all now could lead to acid reflux later. If you end up ordering too much, eat half and take the rest home for another meal. &#8220;Don&#8217;t hesitate to take something to go if it&#8217;s left over,&#8221; Agrawal says. You may be saving yourself a bout of heartburn by not eating the entire meal in one sitting.</li><li><strong>Hold the onions and ketchup.</strong> Choose a fast-food restaurant that allows you to order your food the way you want it. Ask for a burger without onions or ketchup, two foods known to frequently cause heartburn. If you don&#8217;t want <a href="/health-report/heartburn/frequent-heartburn-quiz/questions.aspx">acid reflux symptoms</a>, skip the condiments that cause them.</li><li><strong>Opt for grilled, broiled, or baked foods.</strong> Many fast-food restaurants today offer grilled or broiled chicken. Avoid fried chicken and fish, and limit your consumption of french fries; many people find that fried and fatty foods cause heartburn, and you don&#8217;t want to suffer its symptoms as you&#8217;re driving to your next destination.</li><li><strong>Order salad.</strong> Today most fast-food restaurants have salads on their menus. Just be sure to skip fatty, rich dressings and acidic vinegar dressings, which are likely to cause heartburn. Leave off the tomatoes as well; because they are acidic, they can also lead to reflux.</li><li><strong>Drink water.</strong> Carbonated beverages and caffeine are heartburn triggers for many people. Skip the soda and coffee and ask for a glass of water instead. Most restaurants still offer water at no charge, and your esophagus will thank you.</li><li><strong>Chew gum between meals.</strong> Chewing causes you to produce more saliva, which washes acid from the esophagus, Agrawal says. &#8220;The more saliva you have,&#8221; he says, &#8220;the better.&#8221; Choose fruit-flavored gum, though, as mint flavoring can cause heartburn in some people. </li></ul><br /></p><p>If you&#8217;re in a hurry and hungry, and the only restaurants in sight are fast-food joints, don&#8217;t despair. If you order carefully — skipping the heartburn foods — and eat smaller amounts, you can enjoy your meal without having to worry that it may result in an attack of acid reflux.</p></p></div><div
