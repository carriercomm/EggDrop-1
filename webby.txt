href="/wiki/Japan_Air_Self-Defense_Force" title="Japan Air Self-Defense Force">Japan Air Self-Defense Force</a></td> </tr> </table> <p>The <b>Mitsubishi ATD-X Shinshin</b> is an indigenous Japanese experimental aircraft for testing advanced stealth fighter technologies. It is being developed by the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defense_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Defense (Japan)">Ministry of Defense</a> Technical Research and Development Institute (TRDI) for research purposes. The main contractor of the project is <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Heavy_Industries" title="Mitsubishi Heavy Industries">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries</a>. Many consider this aircraft to be Japan's first domestically made stealth fighter. ATD-X is an acronym meaning "<b>A</b>dvanced <b>T</b>echnology <b>D</b>emonstrator – <b>X</b>". The aircraft is widely known in Japan as Shinshin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">心神</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, meaning "spirit of the heart")</span>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> although the name itself is only an early codename within the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Self-Defense_Forces" title="Japan Self-Defense Forces">Japan Self-Defense Forces</a>; The name is not officially in use.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li> </ul> </div> <p></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsubishi_ATD-X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>At the beginning of the twenty-first century, Japan, seeking to replace its aging fleet of fighter aircraft, began making overtures to the United States on the topic of purchasing several <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin_F-22_Raptor" title="Lockheed Martin F-22 Raptor">Lockheed Martin F-22 Raptor</a> fighters for their own forces.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> However the U.S. Congress had banned the exporting of the aircraft in order to safeguard secrets of the aircraft's technology such as its extensive use of stealth; this rejection necessitated Japan to develop its own modern fighter, to be equipped with stealth features and other advanced systems.</p> <p>A mock-up of the ATD-X was constructed and used to study the <a href="/wiki/Radar_cross_section" title="Radar cross section" class="mw-redirect">radar cross section</a> in France in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> A radio-controlled 1/5 scale model made its first flight in 2006 to gain data on performance at high <a href="/wiki/Angles_of_attack" title="Angles of attack" class="mw-redirect">angles of attack</a> and to test new sensory equipment and self-repairing flight control systems.<sup id="cite_ref-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Following these preliminary steps, the decision was taken in 2007 to push ahead with the multi billion-yen project. At the time of this decision, production was forecast to start roughly 10 years later, around 2017. In 2007, the ATD-X was expected to conduct its maiden flight in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2011, the maiden flight is to take place in 2014 or 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>On July 2014, the TRDI (Technical Research &amp; Development Institute ) released first official photos of the ATD-X prototype, and stated that the aircraft was undergoing tests on ground. Mitsubishi ATD-X shinshin stealth fighter prototype has rolled out, to be fully developed by 2018. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>The ATD-X program will then lead to a F-3 fighter to be produced in 2027.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsubishi_ATD-X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <div class="thumb tright"> <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ATD-X_model_2.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/ATD-X_model_2.JPG/220px-ATD-X_model_2.JPG" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/ATD-X_model_2.JPG/330px-ATD-X_model_2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/ATD-X_model_2.JPG/440px-ATD-X_model_2.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4240" data-file-height="2400" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"> <div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ATD-X_model_2.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> Mitsubishi ATD-X</div> </div> </div> <p>The ATD-X will be used as a technology demonstrator and research prototype to determine whether domestic advanced technologies for a sixth generation <a href="/wiki/Fighter_aircraft" title="Fighter aircraft">fighter aircraft</a> are viable, and is a 1/3 size model of a possible full-production aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The aircraft also features 3D <a href="/wiki/Thrust_vectoring" title="Thrust vectoring">thrust vectoring</a> capability. Thrust is controlled in the ATD-X by the use of 3 paddles on each engine nozzle similar to the system used on the <a href="/wiki/Rockwell_X-31" title="Rockwell X-31" class="mw-redirect">Rockwell X-31</a>, while an axis-symmetric thrust vectoring engine is also being developed for the full scale production model.<sup id="cite_ref-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The nozzles on the prototype appear to be uncovered and might have a slight adverse effect on the aircraft's stealth characteristics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p> <p>Among the features the ATD-X is to have is a <a href="/wiki/Fly-by-optics" title="Fly-by-optics" class="mw-redirect">fly-by-optics</a> flight control system, which by substituting <a href="/wiki/Optical_fibers" title="Optical fibers" class="mw-redirect">optical fibers</a> for wires, allows data to be transferred faster and with immunity to electromagnetic disturbance.<sup id="cite_ref-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Its radar will be an <a href="/wiki/Active_electronically_scanned_array" title="Active electronically scanned array">active electronically scanned array</a> (AESA) radar called the 'Multifunction RF Sensor', which is intended to have broad spectrum agility, capabilities for <a href="/wiki/Electronic_countermeasures" title="Electronic countermeasures" class="mw-redirect">electronic countermeasures</a> (ECM), <a href="/wiki/Electronic_support_measures" title="Electronic support measures" class="mw-redirect">electronic support measures</a> (ESM), communications functions, and possibly even microwave weapon functions.<sup id="cite_ref-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Japanese_Military_Technology_Advances-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>A further feature will be a so-called 'Self Repairing Flight Control Capability' <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">自己修復飛行制御機能</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, which will allow the aircraft to automatically detect failures or damage in its <a href="/wiki/Flight_control_surfaces" title="Flight control surfaces">flight control surfaces</a>, and using the remaining control surfaces, calibrate accordingly to retain controlled flight.<sup id="cite_ref-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>The JASDF is reported to have issued a request for information for engines in the 10 to 20 thousand pound thrust range to power the prototypes while Ishikawajima-Harima Heavy Industries is to provide the engines for the completed fighter.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications_.28ATD-X.29">Specifications (ATD-X)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsubishi_ATD-X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specifications (ATD-X)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0; padding: .2em"><i>Data from</i> Miyakawa et al, 2008<sup id="cite_ref-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Research_of_Flight_Control_System_for_High_Maneuver_Aircraft-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span></p> <ul> <li><b>Crew:</b> 1</li> <li><b>Length:</b> 14.174 meters (46.50 feet)</li> <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">Wingspan</a>:</b> 9.099 meters (29.85 feet)</li> <li><b>Height:</b> 4.514 meters (14.80 feet)</li> <li><b><a href="/wiki/Aircraft_engine" title="Aircraft engine">Powerplant</a>:</b> 2 × <a href="/wiki/IHI_Corporation" title="IHI Corporation">IHI</a> <a
