It is formed from an overgrowth of small veins.<br/></div>              <div class="adSpace300x100" id="adSpace300x100">   <script language="javascript" type="text/javascript">     configureAd("300x100");     if (!showBannerAds) {       document.getElementById("adSpace300x100").style.display = "none";     }   </script>   <script type="text/javascript" src="/visualdxhealth/cobrands/ads.js"></script> </div><div id="DiagnosisTexts" style="visibility:visible;">          <div id="dxWho">           <a name="who"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">Who's At Risk</div>           Cherry hemangiomas are found in individuals of all races and ethnic backgrounds. They occur more frequently with increasing age. <br/></div>          <div id="dxSignsAndSymptoms">           <a name="signs"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">Signs and Symptoms</div>           Cherry hemangiomas may be found on any body location. They range from a small, red, flat dot to a larger, round-topped, bright-cherry-red bump. Sometimes cherry hemangiomas are more purple than red in color. Rarely, a cherry hemangioma lesion demonstrates a dark brown to an almost black color.<br/></div>          <div id="dxSelfCare">           <a name="selfCare"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">Self-Care Guidelines</div>           No self-care is needed except avoiding trauma, which may cause bleeding of the lesions.<br/></div>          <div id="dxMedCare">           <a name="medCare"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">When to Seek Medical Care</div>           Cherry hemangiomas typically require no treatment, although lesions that are irritated or bleeding (most commonly occurring due to injury) usually require removal. Cherry hemangioma lesions can also be removed if they are cosmetically undesirable.<br/></div>          <div id="dxTreatmentsPhysician">           <a name="treatments"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">Treatments Your Physician May Prescribe</div>           Lesions may be surgically removed by cutting away the area (excision), burning away the area (electrocautery), laser, or freezing the area (cryosurgery).<br/><br/><br/></div>          <div id="dxRefs">           <a name="refs"></a>           <br />           <div id="dxReferences"> <a name="references"></a><div class="dxTextTitle">References</div> <br/>Bolognia, Jean L., ed. <i>Dermatology</i>, pp. 1824-1825.
