It is, along with the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Army_National_Guard" title="Kentucky Army National Guard">Kentucky Army National Guard</a>, an element of the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_National_Guard" title="Kentucky National Guard">Kentucky National Guard</a>.</p> <p>As commonwealth militia units, the units in the Kentucky Air National Guard are not in the normal <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> chain of command. They are under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Kentucky" title="Governor of Kentucky">Governor of Kentucky</a> though the office of the <a href="/wiki/State_adjutant_general" title="State adjutant general">Kentucky Adjutant General</a> unless they are federalized by order of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>. The Kentucky Air National Guard is headquartered at <a href="/wiki/Louisville_Air_National_Guard_Base" title="Louisville Air National Guard Base">Louisville Air National Guard Base</a>, and its commander is Brigadier General Mark R. Kraus.</p> <p></p> <div id="toc" class="toc"> <div id="toctitle"> <h2>Contents</h2> </div> <ul> <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Overview"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Overview</span></a></li> <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Components"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Components</span></a></li> <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li> <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li> <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li> </ul> </div> <p></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kentucky_Air_National_Guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Under the "Total Force" concept, Kentucky Air National Guard units are considered to be Air Reserve Components (ARC) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> (USAF). Kentucky ANG units are trained and equipped by the Air Force and are operationally gained by a <a href="/wiki/Major_Command_of_the_USAF" title="Major Command of the USAF" class="mw-redirect">Major Command of the USAF</a> if federalized. In addition, the Kentucky Air National Guard forces are assigned to Air Expeditionary Forces and are subject to deployment tasking orders along with their active duty and Air Force Reserve counterparts in their assigned cycle deployment window.</p> <p>Along with their federal reserve obligations, as commonwealth militia units the elements of the Kentucky ANG are subject to being activated by order of the Governor to provide protection of life and property, and preserve peace, order and public safety. Commonwealth missions include disaster relief in times of earthquakes, hurricanes, floods and forest fires, search and rescue, protection of vital public services, and support to civil defense.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kentucky_Air_National_Guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>The Kentucky Air National Guard consists of the following major unit:</p> <ul> <li><a href="/wiki/123d_Airlift_Wing" title="123d Airlift Wing">123d Airlift Wing</a></li> </ul> <dl> <dd>Established 16 February 1947 (as: <a href="/wiki/165th_Fighter_Squadron" title="165th Fighter Squadron" class="mw-redirect">165th Fighter Squadron</a>); operates: <a href="/wiki/C-130H_Hercules" title="C-130H Hercules" class="mw-redirect">C-130H Hercules</a></dd> <dd>Stationed at: <a href="/wiki/Louisville_Air_National_Guard_Base" title="Louisville Air National Guard Base">Louisville Air National Guard Base</a></dd> <dd>Gained by: <a href="/wiki/Air_Mobility_Command" title="Air Mobility Command">Air Mobility Command</a></dd> </dl> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kentucky_Air_National_Guard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>On 24 May 1946, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a>, in response to dramatic postwar military budget cuts imposed by President <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman" title="Harry S. Truman">Harry S. Truman</a>, allocated inactive unit designations to the <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_Bureau" title="National Guard Bureau">National Guard Bureau</a> for the formation of an Air Force National Guard. These unit designations were allotted and transferred to various State National Guard bureaus to provide them unit designations to re-establish them as Air National Guard units.<sup id="cite_ref-ang60_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ang60-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <div class="thumb tleft"> <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg/220px-165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg/330px-165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg/440px-165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="570" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"> <div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:165th_Fighter_Squadron_F-51D_Formation.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> Formation of Kentucky ANG F-51 Mustangs, 1947</div> </div> </div> <p>The Kentucky Carolina Air National Guard origins date to 9 June 1947 with the establishment of the <b>123d Fighter Group</b> and is oldest unit of the KentuckyCarolina Air National Guard. It was federally recognized and activated at Standiford Field, near Louisville. Its 165th Fighter Squadron, the flying component of the 123d, was equipped with F-51D Mustangs. Its mission was the air defense of the state. 18 September 1947, however, is considered the Kentucky Air National Guard's official birth concurrent with the establishment of the United States Air Force as a separate branch of the United States military under the National Security Act.<sup id="cite_ref-ang60_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ang60-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>With the surprise invasion of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> on 25 June 1950, and the regular military's complete lack of readiness, the ANG was mobilized into federal active duty. The 165th Fighter Squadron was federalized and ordered to active duty on 10 October 1950. The squadron was moved to <a href="/wiki/Godman_Air_Force_Base" title="Godman Air Force Base" class="mw-redirect">Godman Air Force Base</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Knox" title="Fort Knox">Fort Knox</a> along with the 123d Fighter Group. At Godman, the squadron was joined by the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_ANG" title="North Carolina ANG" class="mw-redirect">North Carolina ANG</a> <a href="/wiki/156th_Fighter_Squadron" title="156th Fighter Squadron" class="mw-redirect">156th Fighter Squadron</a> and the <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia_ANG" title="West Virginia ANG" class="mw-redirect">West Virginia ANG</a> <a href="/wiki/167th_Fighter_Squadron" title="167th Fighter Squadron" class="mw-redirect">167th Fighter Squadron</a>. All three squadrons were equipped with F-51Ds.</p> <div class="thumb tright"> <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg/220px-123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg" width="220" height="119" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg/330px-123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg/440px-123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1626" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"> <div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:123rd_Airlift_Wing_personnel_with_C-130Hs_Afghanistan_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> Kentucky ANG personnel with C-130Hs inAfghanistan 2009</div> </div> </div> <p>After over a year of training at Godman AFB, the 123d was re-designated as a Fighter-Bomber Group and deployed to <a href="/wiki/RAF_Manston" title="RAF Manston">RAF Manston</a>, Kent, England to replace the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Air_Command" title="Strategic Air Command">Strategic Air Command</a> <a href="/wiki/12th_Fighter-Escort_Wing" title="12th Fighter-Escort Wing" class="mw-redirect">12th Fighter-Escort Wing</a> which was returned to <a href="/wiki/Bergstrom_AFB" title="Bergstrom AFB" class="mw-redirect">Bergstrom AFB</a>, Texas. In England, the mission of the 123d was to provide fighter escort for <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Air_Command" title="Strategic Air Command">SAC</a> <a href="/wiki/B-50_Superfortress" title="B-50 Superfortress" class="mw-redirect">B-50 Superfortress</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-36_Peacemaker" title="B-36 Peacemaker" class="mw-redirect">B-36 Peacemaker</a> bombers while flying over Western European airspace on their deterrence alert missions. The 123d left their F-51Ds at Godman AFB and the personnel boarded <a href="/wiki/C-124_Globemaster_II" title="C-124 Globemaster II" class="mw-redirect">C-124 Globemaster II</a> transports to England where they initially began conversion training on <a href="/wiki/F-84E_Thunderjet" title="F-84E Thunderjet" class="mw-redirect">F-84E Thunderjets</a>.</p> <p>Today, the 123d Airlift Wing (123 AW) provides worldwide theater airlift for U.S. military and humanitarian operations. The wing is equipped with eight C-130H model aircraft. Multiple groups, squadrons and flights carry out the unit's mission by providing administrative and logistical support, including airlift operations, combat control, pararescue, maintenance, supply, transportation, contracting, communications, civil engineering, personnel, base services, security forces and medical functions.</p> <p>After the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11th, 2001 terrorist attacks on the United States</a>, elements of every Air National Guard unit in Kentucky has been activated in support of the <a href="/wiki/Global_War_on_Terrorism" title="Global War on Terrorism" class="mw-redirect">Global War on Terrorism</a>. Flight crews, aircraft maintenance personnel, communications technicians, air controllers and air security personnel were engaged in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Noble_Eagle" title="Operation Noble Eagle">Operation Noble Eagle</a> air defense overflights of major United States cities. Also, Kentucky ANG units have been deployed overseas as part of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Enduring_Freedom" title="Operation Enduring Freedom">Operation Enduring Freedom</a> in Afghanistan and <a
