<div id="node-36238" class="node"> <div>   <button class="left_arrow floatleft left_arrow_solo home" id="back-36238"></button>   <h2 class="story-title">       Filbert gets early start in campaign for town justice post    </h2> </div> <div id="font-box">Text: <span id="font-box-s">T</span> <input type="range" id="fontsize" name="fontsize" value="1" min="1" max="3" /> <span id="font-box-l">T</span></div> <div class="content">   <div class="field field-type-text field-field-oht-author">     <div class="field-items">             <div class="field-item odd">                     by JENNIFER WATERS        </div>               <div class="field-item even">                     Editor        </div>         </div> </div> <p><span class="inline inline-left"><a href="/node/36237"><img src="http://m.westsenecabee.com/sites/www.westsenecabee.com/files/images/2014-12-18/22p1.preview.jpg" alt=" Filbert " title=" Filbert "  class="image image-preview " width="167" height="250" /></a><span class="caption" style="width: 165px;"> Filbert </span></span>Attorney Shannon Filbert has officially launched her campaign to serve as the next West Seneca town justice. Filbert is seeking the seat to be vacated by Judge Dale McCabe.</p> <p>&ldquo;I am excited about the opportunity to continue to serve the residents of West Seneca,&rdquo; Filbert said. &ldquo;As an attorney practicing before several courts on a daily basis, I will bring real-world legal experience, fairness and compassion to the bench.&rdquo;</p> <p>Filbert has been serving as a West Seneca town prosecutor since 2010, handling thousands of cases in Town Court. She is also an attorney for the Children&rsquo;s Unit at the Legal Aid Bureau of Buffalo Inc., representing severely abused and neglected children.</p> <p>She has handled cases in all areas of family law and served as court-appointed counsel for young parents and children in Family Court.</p> <p>Additionally, she serves as an attorney for the Erie County Bar Association Aid to Indigent Prisoner Society Inc. Assigned Counsel Program.</p> <p> In each of these roles, Filbert said, she has experienced firsthand the effects the court system has on Erie County&rsquo;s families and children.</p> <p> At present, Filbert is a member of both the Prosecutors Association of Western New York and the New York State Defenders Association. Filbert said her dual experience prosecuting and defending cases has allowed her to cultivate a balanced point of view to preside as West Seneca town justice.</p> <p>&ldquo;My dedication and experience on both sides of the law, as a prosecutor and defense attorney, has provided me with a broad perspective to bring to the bench,&rdquo; she said. </p><div class="island-zone"><!--island--></div></p> <p>According to Filbert, her devotion to the people of West Seneca and Western New York is partly in recognition of the support she received after a life-changing motor vehicle accident in 1998. She has since been quadriplegic.</p> <p>With the help of the community, Filbert&rsquo;s parents made their home wheelchair-accessible, allowing her to remain in the West Seneca School District and graduate on time from West Seneca East Senior High School. </p> <p>Filbert continued her education and earned both her undergraduate and law degrees from the University at Buffalo.</p> <p>Her most recent accomplishment was becoming a West Seneca homeowner, as she built a handicapped-accessible ranch home.</p> <p>Filbert currently serves on the board of directors for the Wheels with Wings Foundation, a not-for-profit organization dedicated to assisting individuals who have suffered a spinal cord injury. She is the chairperson of the Wheels with Wings Grant Committee and volunteers her time working to improve the lives of people with spinal cord injury so they may become independent and productive members of society.</p> <p>&ldquo;My real-world legal experience as a practicing attorney and life path have provided me with a unique perspective to serve our community,&rdquo; she said.</p> <p>Filbert has not decided which party line she will run on at this time.</p> <p>The primary election will be held on Tuesday, Sept. 15, and the general election on Tuesday, Nov. 3. </p><p>email: <a
