      <p>Prosecutors Monday dropped charges against a 19-year-old Katy man who had been accused of plotting a school shooting spree, his lawyer said.</p><p><a href="/search/?action=search&channel=news%2Fhouston-texas%2Fhouston&inlineLink=1&searchindex=gsa&query=%22Patrick+Joseph+Hudson%22">Patrick Joseph Hudson</a>, who was arrested in October and held on a $100,000 bail, was charged with making a terroristic threat after the FBI got a tip that someone using the name "SS Patrick," in an online chat forum was making threatening comments regarding a school shooting.</p><p>After further investigation, said defense lawyer <a href="/search/?action=search&channel=news%2Fhouston-texas%2Fhouston&inlineLink=1&searchindex=gsa&query=%22Laine+Lindsey%22">Laine Lindsey</a>, the authorities dismissed the case for insufficient evidence.</p><p>"There was no threat, this was a conversation," Lindsey said. "It has to be a threat against some identifiable person, place or thing."</p><p>He said authorities found no evidence on his computer that he intended to harm anyone.</p><p /><p /><p /><p />
