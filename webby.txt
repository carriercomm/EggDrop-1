datetime="2014-11-26T11:40:44Z">  Wednesday, November 26, 2014 05:29AM  </time>   </div>       <div class="body-text">          <div class="dateline">MESA COUNTY, CO -- </div>     A man who says he was just trying to "lighten the holiday spirit" is now facing serious charges. His crime: Pointing a banana at police.<br><br>The <a target="" rel="" href="http://bit.ly/1rqFiVE">Grand Junction Daily Sentinel</a> reports Nathen Rolf Channing, 27, was arrested after he pointed a banana at police officers. Police initially thought the banana was a gun.<br><br>According to part of an arrest affidavit released to <a target="" rel="" href="http://kdvr.com/2014/11/25/man-arrested-for-pointing-banana-at-deputies-near-grand-junction/">KDVR-TV</a>, Mesa County deputies Joshua Bunch and Donald Love said they feared for their lives after they "observed what appeared to be a yellow tube with a black center."<br><br>"Nathen drew the object in the same manner someone would draw a standard handgun from a concealed holster," Bunch stated in the affidavit, saying both he Love thought the banana was a gun.<br><br>Deputy Love then began to draw his service weapon when Channing yelled, "It's a banana!"<br><br>Bunch wrote in the affidavit that he has seen handguns in many shapes and colors and was following his training.<br><br>The deputies say Channing told them he was doing a trial run for a planned YouTube video and he thought it would be a "funny joke." It may be a joke that went too far. Channing now faces two counts of felony menacing. A conviction could get him three years in prison and a fine of up to $100,000.<br><br><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i>    <div class="mmn-link"><a href="/map/">Map My
