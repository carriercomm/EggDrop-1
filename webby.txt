The first auction round ends on Monday Dec 15 at 7:45 AM PST with a new round ending every 45 minutes until the last auction round ends on Thursday Dec 18 10AM PST. One of each title will be awarded to the top bidder at the end of each round. That means 100 copies of nearly 2,000 titles are up for auction for a grand total of up to 200,000.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How do I win games/auctions?</div>       <div class="faq_a">Bid the most amount of Gems in an auction round between the dates above to win. Once the auction rounds start on Monday, every 45 minutes the top bidder wins a game and all other bids carry over to the next round.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How do I make Steam Gems?</div>       <div class="faq_a">You can make Gems by recycling Steam Community Items from <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/my/inventory">your Inventory</a>. Go to your Inventory, select the Steam tab, and choose "Community" from the drop down to view eligible items. From there you can see the Gem value of each item and a button to recycle it. In addition to bidding, Gems can also be used to <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/tradingcards/boostercreator/">create Steam Trading Card Booster Packs</a>.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How do I bid?</div>       <div class="faq_a">You'll need Steam Gems to bid, which you can get by recycling Steam Community Items from <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/my/inventory">your Inventory</a>. Select a game from the Auctions list and enter your bid. Gems are removed from your Inventory when they are used to bid and will be returned to your Inventory if the bid is canceled.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How do I increase or cancel my bid?</div>       <div class="faq_a">From the My Bids section above, click on the bid you want to cancel. This will take you to that games auction detail page where you have the options to increase or cancel your bid. Your bid can be canceled at any time unless it is the top bid.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How long is my bid good for?</div>       <div class="faq_a">Unless you cancel your bid or you are the top bidder and win the game, your bid will carry over after each auction round. After all auction rounds are completed on Thursday Dec 18 10AM PST, all outstanding bids are canceled and the Gems are returned to your Inventory.</div>      </div>      <div class="faq_right">       <div class="faq_q">How do I know if I have won?</div>       <div class="faq_a">You can track your current bids, top bids, and winning bids in the My Bids section above. If you win an auction, you'll receive it as a <a href="https://support.steampowered.com/kb_article.php?p_faqid=549">Gift</a> that you can add to your Library or Inventory. You'll also receive an email notification from Steam Support.</div>       <div class="faq_q">What can I bid on?</div>       <div class="faq_a">There are up to 2,000 different games up for auction. There's also a special profile item up for auction called the <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/auction/item/1890-Golden-Profile">2014 Holiday Profile</a>.</div>       <div class="faq_q">What happens to Steam Gems after the Holiday Auction ends?</div>       <div class="faq_a">Gems will not disappear from your Inventory after the Holiday Auction event ends. You can continue making and using it to <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/tradingcards/boostercreator/">create Booster Packs</a>.</div>       <div class="faq_q">How do I get the 2014 Holiday Auction badge?</div>       <div class="faq_a">The Auction Badge has three levels that correspond to Auction participation. Create Gems to earn level 1, place a bid to earn level 2, and win an auction round to earn the final level.</div>       <div class="faq_q">Can I place a "max bid"?</div>       <div class="faq_a">Your bid should be the most you are willing to spend. Steam will automatically increase your bid up to that amount to keep yours the highest bid. Steam will never exceed your bid unless you increase it.</div>       <div class="faq_q">Can I transfer my Steam Gems and Booster Packs?</div>       <div class="faq_a">Yes, these are Steam Inventory items that are transferable in the normal ways:  <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/my/tradeoffers/">trading</a> or selling on the <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/market/">Steam Community Market</a>.
