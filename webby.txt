<p><a href="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/nexus2cee_QFolio_Combo_BlkGry_540c57ev5kn.png"></a>The new <a href="http://www.androidpolice.com/2014/10/28/verizon-makes-the-droid-turbo-official-a-beefed-up-moto-x-with-a-monster-battery-launching-thursday-for-200-on-contract/">DROID Turbo for Verizon</a> is a beast of a phone, but Motorola isn't letting it stand alone. The company also announced a proprietary see-through case for the device, with the somewhat benign name "<a href="http://www.motorola.com/us/consumers/accessories-protection-droid/Droid-Turbo-Flip-Case/droid-turbo-flip-case.html" class="external" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Droid Turbo Flip Case</a>." It's a pretty standard full-body flip case offered in either leather or nylon, but it includes a screen cutout and a transparent cover that lets you view the phone's active notifications, or even answer a call or take a photo, without opening the case. So yeah, it's basically like Samsung's S-View cases and their imitators.</p> <p align="center"><a href="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/nexus2cee_QFolio_Combo_BlkGry_540c57ev5kn1.png"><img style="display: inline;" title="QFolio_Combo_BlkGry_540c57ev5kn" src="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/nexus2cee_QFolio_Combo_BlkGry_540c57ev5kn_thumb1.png" alt="QFolio_Combo_BlkGry_540c57ev5kn" width="540" height="540"></a></p> <p>Though the Turbo's Flip Case isn't actually available to order yet, Motorola has already released an app to support its see-through functionality. The phone's Moto X-style notifications should work without any extra software, but presumably formatting the notifications, dialer, and camera apps for the cutout requires a little development footwork. According to the app description, pressing the power key while the case is closed will quickly show you the time, your next alarm, and the current battery level.
