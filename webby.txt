with blood-red paint disrupted <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/12/10/malala-yousafzai-nobel-peace-prize/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">Malala Yousafzai's Nobel Peace Prize</a> acceptance ceremony on Wednesday, as the 17-year-old Pakistani activist stood on stage in Oslo, Norway.</p> <div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/10/10/malala-nobel-moments/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">Nobel Prize Winner Malala Yousafzai's Most Inspiring Moments</a></p></div> <p>The man, who had a camera hanging from his neck, yelled, "Please, Malala, they are killing us. Don't forget Mexico!" He was promptly arrested, according to Oslo police.</p> <p>As the man was escorted out by three security guards, he shouted, "Viva Mexico!"</p> <p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/ApKtqw-BVEE?enablejsapi=1&amp;" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p> <p>The appeal is most likely a reference to the disappearance and suspected massacre of 43 college students from a rural teachers college in Guerrero state — and the government's <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/10/23/protests-mexico-missing-students/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">failure to explain what exactly happened</a> to them.</p> <p>Mexico has been hampered by <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/11/09/mexico-protests/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">increasingly violent protests</a> since the incident.</p> <p>Investigators say Iguala Mayor Jose Luis Abarca and his wife, Maria de los Angeles Pineda Villa, ordered police to confront the students, who had gone to Iguala to raise money and had commandeered passenger buses for their use. The couple reportedly feared the students would disrupt an event being led by Pineda Villa.</p> <p>Iguala police fired on the students in two incidents, killing six people. Officers then allegedly turned over 43 arrested students to a local drug gang who, some say, <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/11/07/suspects-admit-to-iguala-murders/">murdered them</a>, burned their bodies and threw the remains into a mass grave and river.</p> <p>"I am angry, sad and Mexican society is, too," Mexico's attorney general <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/11/07/suspects-admit-to-iguala-murders/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">said of the case</a> last month.</p> <p><em>Additional reporting by The Associated Press</em> <em>Translation by <a href="https://twitter.com/tradubeledi" target="_blank">Brenda L.
