During the 18-minute filmed encounter, Eugene (who was himself stripped nude) accused Poppo of stealing his Bible, beat him unconscious, removed Poppo's pants, and bit off most of Poppo's face above the beard, including his left eye, leaving him blind in both.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> As a result of the incident's shocking nature and subsequent worldwide media coverage, Eugene came to be dubbed the "Miami Zombie" as well as the "Causeway Cannibal".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The attack ended when Eugene was fatally shot by a <a href="/wiki/Miami_Police_Department" title="Miami Police Department">Miami police</a> officer.</p> <p>Although friends and family have filled in details of Eugene's life, the reason for the attack remains unclear. Eugene, 31, employed at a car wash at the time, was a divorced former high school football player with a series of petty criminal arrests from age 16 until most recently in 2009. Poppo, 65, a graduate of Manhattan's <a href="/wiki/Stuyvesant_High_School" title="Stuyvesant High School">Stuyvesant High School</a>, was homeless and had long been presumed dead by his estranged family.</p> <p>While police sources speculated that the use of a street drug like "<a href="/wiki/Bath_salts_(drug)" title="Bath salts (drug)">bath salts</a>" might have been a factor,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> experts have expressed doubt toward this speculation as toxicology reports were only able to identify <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" title="Marijuana" class="mw-redirect">marijuana</a> in his system, leaving the ultimate cause of Eugene's behavior to remain unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a Attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <table class="mbox-small plainlinks" style="border:1px solid #aaa;background-color:#f9f9f9"> <tr> <td class="mbox-image"><img related news: <i><b><a href="//en.wikinews.org/wiki/Miami_police_shoot,_kill_naked_man_chewing_face_of_male_victim" class="extiw" title="wikinews:Miami police shoot, kill naked man chewing face of Beach" class="mw-redirect">Miami Beach</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Urban_Beach_Week" title="Urban Beach Week">Urban Beach Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> His flag-draped purple <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Caprice" title="Chevrolet Caprice">Chevrolet Caprice</a> eventually became disabled, and, after spending 30–40 minutes filmed on security video in and around the car,<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> according to eyewitnesses he abandoned it about noon and began to cross the three-mile long span of the <a href="/wiki/MacArthur_Causeway" title="MacArthur Causeway">MacArthur Causeway</a>, stripping himself of his clothing and disposing of his <a href="/wiki/Driver%27s_license" title="Driver's license">driver’s license</a> as he advanced westward.<sup id="cite_ref-DailyNewsZombie_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DailyNewsZombie-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> His vehicle was eventually discovered and towed by <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach_Police_Department" title="Miami Beach Police Department">Miami Beach police</a>. Inside the car, police discovered a bible and five empty water bottles, which they believe were recently consumed.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Eugene, eventually completely naked, having discarded even his shoes and lastly his <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> at the crime scene,<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> encountered Poppo at approximately 1:55&#160;pm. Poppo had been lying underneath an elevated <a href="/wiki/Metromover" title="Metromover">Metromover</a> railway when Eugene began to pummel him, strip him of his pants, and bite his face. The attack unfolded at the west end of the <a href="/wiki/MacArthur_Causeway" title="MacArthur Causeway">MacArthur Causeway</a>, near the headquarters of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Miami_Herald" title="The Miami Herald">The Miami Herald</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Omni_(Miami)" title="Omni (Miami)">Omni</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Miami" title="Downtown Miami">Downtown Miami</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It was at first believed that neither Eugene nor Poppo<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> knew the other before their encounter, until a July 2012 publication revealed that Eugene had met Poppo while working for the homeless community of Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> A passing cyclist, Larry Vega, came upon the scene and alerted authorities via <a href="/wiki/9-1-1" title="9-1-1">9-1-1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> A few minutes later, <a href="/wiki/Miami_Police_Department" title="Miami Police Department">Miami Police Department</a> officer Jose Ramirez<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> arrived and, after doing a <a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/double_take" class="extiw" title="wikt:double take">double take</a> at the spectacle,<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> warned Eugene to desist from attacking Poppo. Eugene ignored the officer's warnings and, instead, reportedly growled at him, then resumed biting his victim.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The attack ended at 2:13 PM with Officer Ramirez shooting Eugene once at first and then another four times when that proved ineffective. The ordeal had been captured by a security camera on <i>The Miami Herald</i> building.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The surveillance video shows that the attack continued for 18 minutes before help arrived.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miami_cannibal_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>Poppo, age 65, was admitted into <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Memorial_Hospital" title="Jackson Memorial Hospital">Jackson Memorial Hospital</a>, critically injured, with 75–80% of his face above the beard missing<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and his left eye gouged out in the attack.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> He underwent facial reconstruction surgeries that took months to complete and remained permanently disfigured and blind.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> To combat the costs, a fund was set up and has collected $100,700 since July 17, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Poppo, who lost sight in both his eyes, spoke to police, whom he thanked for saving his life, on July 19, explaining that Eugene, whom he hardly knew, approached him in a friendly manner but then, complaining he couldn't "score" at the beach and "souped up on something", started to talk about how they were going to die, accused Poppo of stealing his bible, and, suddenly and without provocation, attacked and strangled him with wrestling holds, and then "plucked out" both his eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>In the face of this, combined with <a href="/wiki/Luka_Magnotta" title="Luka Magnotta">Luka Magnotta</a>'s cannibalistic behavior and the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Kujoe_Bonsafo_Agyei-Kodie" title="Murder of Kujoe Bonsafo Agyei-Kodie">murder of Kujoe Bonsafo Agyei-Kodie</a>, people began to believe that the <a href="/wiki/Zombie_apocalypse" title="Zombie apocalypse" class="mw-redirect">zombie apocalypse</a> was at hand.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> As a result, the <a href="/wiki/Centers_for_Disease_Control_and_Prevention" title="Centers for Disease Control and Prevention">Centers for Disease Control and Prevention</a> released a statement saying that the "CDC does not know of a virus or condition that would reanimate the dead (or one that would present zombie-like symptoms)."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inconclusive_investigation">Inconclusive investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miami_cannibal_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Inconclusive investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>Although the autopsy revealed no human flesh in Eugene's stomach, a number of undigested pills were discovered that have not been identified.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Although police sources had speculated that the street drug "<a href="/wiki/Bath_salts_(drug)" title="Bath salts (drug)">bath salts</a>" might have been involved, preliminary toxicology reports were positive only for the presence of <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" title="Marijuana" class="mw-redirect">marijuana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Authorities have not necessarily found the negative results conclusive; Broward Sheriff Al Lamberti has expressed a belief that some new drug not yet tested for played a role; nationally noted toxicologist Barry Logan said Eugene's behavior was consistent with "bath salts" and that toxicologists "are not testing for everything that may be out there"; and the director of toxicology at the University of Florida, Dr. Bruce Goldberger, said, "We are not incompetent...We have the tools, we have the sophistication and know-how. But the field is evolving so rapidly it is hard for us to keep track. Hospital">Jackson Memorial Hospital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> He was of <a Eugene never made contact with his father, who died when he was six.<sup id="cite_ref-earlylife_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlylife-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> As a child he attended the Bethel Evangelical Baptist Church with his families on most Sundays.<sup id="cite_ref-earlylife_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlylife-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> Eugene attended <a href="/wiki/North_Miami_Beach_High_School" title="North Miami Beach High School">North Miami Beach High School</a> in the 10th and 11th grades,<sup id="cite_ref-cbsmiami_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbsmiami-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> and he played on its football team in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-thv_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thv-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> At the age of 17, he transferred to <a href="/wiki/North_Miami_High_School" title="North Miami High School">North Miami High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stepfather_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stepfather-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> After graduating, Eugene acquired money from such sources as selling <a href="/wiki/CD" title="CD" class="mw-redirect">CDs</a> and jobs at <a href="/wiki/McDonald%E2%80%99s" title="McDonald’s" class="mw-redirect">McDonald’s</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Telemarketing" title="Telemarketing">telemarketing</a>. His last recorded employment was washing cars at a local automobile dealership.<sup id="cite_ref-MiamiHerald_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MiamiHerald-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> Eugene had expressed interest in establishing his own mobile <a href="/wiki/Car_wash" title="Car wash">car wash</a> business.<sup id="cite_ref-MiamiHerald_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MiamiHerald-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-man_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-man-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Eugene's marriage to Jenny Ductant, from 2005 to 2008, ended in divorce, with Ductant reporting that there had been domestic violence in the relationship. The divorce was granted on January 8, 2008. They had no children from their relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-wptv_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wptv-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> Eugene then became estranged from Ductant until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-local10_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-local10-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Between his divorce proceedings, he met Rikkia Cross in 2007, with whom he remained until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-earlylife_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earlylife-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_history">Criminal history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miami_cannibal_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criminal history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>Eugene was arrested eight times from the age of 16, with the first arrest being for an assault in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-wptv_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wptv-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eugene_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eugene-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> Another assault occurred on February 25, 2004, when Eugene broke a table, smashed items around the house, and pushed his mother out of the kitchen. Afterward, Eugene's mother Ruth told officers that he had said, "I'll put a gun to your head and kill you." This crime led to his serving probation for resisting an officer without violence.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> The remaining charges were mainly related to marijuana, which he had expressed a desire to quit.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> His last
