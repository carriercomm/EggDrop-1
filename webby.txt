class="container-article" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">    <article class="hl-article-toc">      <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_1"><a name="Description"></a>What Is Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome? </h2><div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_1"> <p>Alcohol withdrawal syndrome (AWS)  is the name for the symptoms that occur when a heavy drinker suddenly stops or  significantly reduces their alcohol intake. With AWS, you may experience a combination of physical and emotional symptoms, from mild anxiety  and fatigue to nausea. Some symptoms of  AWS are as severe as hallucinations and seizures. At its most extreme, AWS can  be life-threatening. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_2"><a name="Causes"></a>What Causes Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome? </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_2"> <p>Excessive drinking excites the <A id="hllink54" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/human-body-maps/head-nervous-system">nervous system</A>. If you drink daily, over time your body becomes dependent on alcohol. When this happens, your central nervous system can no longer adapt easily to the lack of alcohol.  If you suddenly stop drinking, or significantly reduce the amount of alcohol you drink, it can cause a number of  symptoms. These include <A id="hllink14" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/anxiety">anxiety</A>, <A id="hllink70" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/insomnia">insomnia</A>, heart rate changes, increased blood pressure, and <A id="hllink73" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/delirium">delirium</A>. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_3"><a name="Risk"></a>Who Is at Risk for Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome?  </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_3"> <p>People who are addicted to alcohol, or who drink heavily on a regular basis and cannot  gradually cut down, are at high risk for AWS. AWS is more common in adults,  but children and teenagers who drink excessively may also experience the  symptoms. You are also at risk for AWS if you have previously had withdrawal  symptoms or needed medical detox for a drinking problem. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_4"><a name="Symptoms"></a>What Are the Symptoms of Alcohol Withdrawal  Syndrome? </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_4"> <p>Symptoms of AWS may appear  anywhere from six hours to a few days after your last drink. These usually  include at least two of the following:</p> <ul>   <li>tremors</li>   <li>anxiety</li>   <li>nausea and/or <A id="hllink107" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/vomiting">vomiting</A></li>   <li>headache</li>   <li>increased heart rate </li>   <li>sweating</li>   <li>irritability</li>   <li>confusion</li>   <li>insomnia</li>   <li>nightmares</li> </ul> <p>Symptoms may worsen over two to  three days and persist for weeks. They may be more noticeable when you wake up  with less alcohol in your blood.</p> <p>The most severe type of  withdrawal syndrome is known as delirium tremens. Its symptoms include:</p> <ul type="disc">   <li>extreme confusion and agitation</li><li>fever</li><li>seizures</li><li>tactile <A id="hllink34" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/hallucinations">hallucinations</A> (e.g., itching, burning, and  numbness)</li><li>auditory hallucinations (e.g., hearing non-existent  sounds)</li><li>visual hallucinations (e.g., seeing non-existent  images)</li></ul><p>If you have severe AWS symptoms, it is a medical emergency. Call for help or go to the emergency room. A high fever, hallucinations, and heart disturbances are all reasons to seek immediate help.</p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_5"><a name="Diagnosis"></a>How Is Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome Diagnosed? </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_5"> <p>Your doctor will review your  medical history, ask about your symptoms, and conduct a physical exam. Some  signs your doctor will look for include:</p> <ul type="disc">   <li>hand tremors</li>   <li>an irregular heart rate</li>   <li>dehydration</li>   <li>fever </li> </ul> <p>Your doctor may also perform a  toxicology screen. This tests how much alcohol is in your body. </p> <p>The Clinical Institute Withdrawal  Assessment for Alcohol (CIWA-Ar) is a series of questions used to measure AWS.  Your doctor may use this test to diagnose AWS. It can also be used to determine  the severity of your symptoms. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_6"><a name="Treatment"></a>How Is Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome Treated? </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_6"> <p>Treatment for AWS depends on how  severe your symptoms are. Some people can be treated at home, but others may need supervised care in a hospital setting to avoid potentially dangerous complications. </p> <p>The first goal of treatment is to  keep you comfortable by managing your symptoms.  Alcohol counseling is another important treatment goal. Your doctor will want  you to stop drinking as quickly and safely as possible.</p> <h3>Home Care</h3> <p>Mild symptoms of AWS can often be treated at home. A relative or friend must stay with you to monitor  your condition. Their job is to make sure you go to counseling and visit the  doctor regularly for routine blood tests. You may also need tests for  alcohol-related medical problems. </p> <p>If your home environment is not  helpful for staying sober, talk to your doctor. He may be able to help you find  a place to stay until you recover. </p> <h3>Hospitalization </h3> <p>If your symptoms are more severe,  you may need hospitalization. This is so a doctor can monitor your condition and manage any complications.  You may require IV fluids to prevent <A id="hllink213" class="hllink hllink-auto" href="http://www.healthline.com/health/dehydration">dehydration</A> and medications to help ease your symptoms.</p> <h3>Medication</h3> <p>Symptoms of AWS are often treated with  sedatives (benzodiazepines). Once withdrawal is complete, additional medications and supplements may be needed to address complications and nutritional deficiences that occur as a result of chronic alcohol use. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_7"><a name="Prognosis"></a>Outlook for Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_7"> <p>Most people with AWS recover  fully. If you stop drinking, get treatment, and are otherwise healthy, the outlook is usually good. However, sleep disturbances, irritability, and  fatigue may continue for months.</p> <p>If AWS has advanced to delirium  tremens, it can be fatal. If you begin experiencing severe symptoms of AWS, it is important to seek immediate medical attention. The faster you begin treatment, the better your chances are of avoiding life-threatening complications. </p> </div> <h2 class="content_head" id="TOC_TITLE_HDR_8"><a name="Prevention"></a>Preventing Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome </h2> <div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_8"> <p>The best way to prevent AWS is to  avoid regular heavy drinking. If you are already dependent on alcohol, seek  counseling and medical care. The goal is to safely and gradually lessen your dependence on alcohol so that  you can resume a healthy life. </p> </div></article>   <div class="span12 box-share-help-feedback">       <div
