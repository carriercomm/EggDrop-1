        </div>                         <div id='article_main' class="asset-main">                                                          <!-- AP Content -->                             <div class="entry-content">                                 <p>WATERLOO - A 15-year-old girl was who was threatened by a stranger with a knife in a mall restroom forgave her attacker, the girl's father said.</p> <p>"I'm very proud of my daughter for being able to do that," the father told assailant Thomas James Boggs, 19.</p> <p>"We do forgive you, Thomas," the victim's father said.</p> <p>Authorities said Boggs followed the girl into the women's restroom at Crossroads Center, showed her a knife and told her he wasn't going to let her leave.</p> <p>The girl escaped, cutting her hand as she fled, and summoned help.</p> <p>Boggs, who pleaded guilty to assault with intent to commit sex abuse, going armed, assault while participating in a felony and carrying weapons, sniffled through his apology in court Friday.</p> <p>"I'm sorry. I didn't know why I did what I did. I lashed out at an innocent person," Boggs said.</p> <p>But the apology and forgiveness had little effect on Boggs's punishment.</p> <p>Judge Kellyann Lekar spared Boggs only two years from his maximum possible sentence, sending him to prison for a series of consecutive prison stints totaling 15 years.</p> <p>She said the significant prison sentence would allow him to get help while incarcerated.</p> <p>Public defender Andrea Dryer had asked for a five-year sentence, noting Boggs's youth and lack of prior criminal record.</p> <p>Assistant County Attorney Joel Dalrymple, who had asked for 17 years behind bars, said the crime was particularly heinous.</p> <p>"But for her strength and her ability to get away, horrible things were about to happen to her," Dalrymple said.</p> <p>He noted that after Boggs was caught, he told police he intended to rape the girl in front of the bathroom mirror so she could watch her expressions during the attack.</p> <p>Boggs, a former New Mexico resident who recently moved to Cedar Falls to live with relatives, had been fired from his job stocking shelves at the Waverly Wal-Mart earlier on the day of the attack, Dryer said.</p> <p>"On this particular day, something in him just snapped," she said, noting friends and family members described him as a kind, caring person and were shocked when they heard of his arrest.</p> <p>"They have not seen him like this," she said.</p> <p>When Boggs was detained by mall security guards, he banged his own head into a wall and said he wanted to shoot himself, Dryer said.</p> <p>Boggs was ordered to receive sex offender treatment, and he will have to register as a sex offender when he is released. He also will have 10 years of parole for the assault with intent to commit sex abuse charge.</p> <p>Contact Jeff Reinitz at (319) 291-1578 or <a href= "mailto:jeff.reinitz@wcfcourier.com">jeff.reinitz@wcfcourier.com</a>.</p>                              </div>                         </div>                     </div>                    </div>                    <script type="text/javascript">                        if( showSurvey )                        {                            try { _402_Show(); } catch(e){}                        }                    </script>                    <style type="text/css">                        .t402-inline-block {                             z-index: 99!important;                            float:left!important;                            width:375px!important;                        }                    </style>                                          <!-- AP Licence -->                     <p class="terms"><small><span class="muted" rel="item-license" id="license-562f18e9-e965-5d38-93b8-de4400d497ae">Copyright 2014 Waterloo Cedar Falls Courier.
