class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bast%C3%B8y_Prison.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> Bastøy Prison from the air</div> </div> </div> <p><b>Bastøy Prison</b> (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <span lang="no" xml:lang="no"><i>Bastøy landsfengsel</i></span>) is a minimum-security <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prison</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bast%C3%B8y_Island" title="Bastøy Island" class="mw-redirect">Bastøy Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, about 75&#160;kilometres (46&#160;mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>. The prison is on a 2.6&#160;square kilometre (1&#160;sq&#160;mi) island and hosts 115 <a href="/wiki/Incarceration" title="Incarceration" class="mw-redirect">inmates</a>. Arne Kvernvik-Nilsen,<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_2012-05-18_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_2012-05-18-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> governor of the prison, leads a staff of 69 prison employees. Of this staff, only five employees remain on the island overnight.<sup id="cite_ref-ENN_2006-03-28_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ENN_2006-03-28-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The prison is about one hour commuting distance from Oslo.<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_2011-08-03_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN_2011-08-03-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Once a prison colony for <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_delinquency" title="Juvenile delinquency">young boys</a>, the facility now is trying to become "the first ecological prison in the world".<sup id="cite_ref-ENN_2006-03-28_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ENN_2006-03-28-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Reoffending rates have been reported at 16%, compared to a European average of around 70%.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Inmates are housed in wooden cottages and work the <a href="/wiki/Prison_farm" title="Prison farm">prison farm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shishkin_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shishkin-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> During their free time, inmates have access to <a href="/wiki/Horseback_riding" title="Horseback riding" class="mw-redirect">horseback riding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ENN_2006-03-28_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ENN_2006-03-28-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hundley_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hundley-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The only access to the prison is from a <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> that departs from <a href="/wiki/Horten" title="Horten">Horten</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a id="Bast.C3.B8y_Boys.27_Home_Insurrection">Bastøy Boys' Home Insurrection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bast%C3%B8y_Prison&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bastøy Boys&#039; Home Insurrection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Before the current prison, the island was occupied by a juvenile detention centre, Bastøy Boys' Home. In 1915, it was the site of an insurrection by the boys which was suppressed by the Norwegian military; this event was later dramatised in the 2010 film, <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_Devil%27s_Island" title="King of Devil's Island">King of Devil's Island</a></i>. The Boys' Home was taken over by the Norwegian government in 1953 and shut down in 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_2011-12-16_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN_2011-12-16-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>The rebellion occurred on 20 May 1915, when between 30 and 40 boys rallied around four youths who had escaped and been recaptured. The group refused to work, armed themselves with farming tools and stones, cut the telephone lines and then burned down the barn with stolen matches and cigars. The same barn had been burned down two years before.</p> <p>When instructors and guards failed to quell the riot, the military was called in. Over a hundred troops stormed the island. Also on scene were two seaplanes, two submarines, and the armored ship "Norway" from the naval base <a href="/wiki/Karljohansvern" title="Karljohansvern">Karljohansvern</a> in Horten. Several of the boys escaped into the forest but were later recaptured. Officials identified the ringleader of the rioters as a newly arrived 18-year-old "gypsy boy" plumbing apprentice from <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Christiania</a>. Three others were identified as his accomplices.</p> <p>The 1915 uprising did not end the school's strict disciplinarian methods which continued until 1953 when the Ministry of Social Affairs took over operations. The school was shut down on 1 October 1970. Bastøy Island, once famous for its brutal reformatory, is now famous for the progressive Bastøy Prison founded in 1982 where inmates live in cottages, dine on food cooked by a chef, and enjoy a <a href="/wiki/Sauna" title="Sauna">sauna</a>, tennis courts and horseback riding.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prison">Prison</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bast%C3%B8y_Prison&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prison">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>In 2014, the prison was awarded the 2014 Blanche Major Reconciliation Prize for "promoting human values and tolerance".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bast%C3%B8y_Prison&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>The prison colony was featured in the DVD extras for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moore" title="Michael Moore">Michael Moore</a>'s 2007 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Sicko" title="Sicko">Sicko</a></i>.
