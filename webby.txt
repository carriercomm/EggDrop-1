HAVE ISSUED A SPECIAL WEATHER STATEMENT AS IT IS STILL A BIT EARLY TO DETERMINE IF WARNING OR ADVISORY CONDITIONS WILL OCCUR ON THE NIGHT BEFORE CHRISTMAS AND CHRISTMAS DAY. TIMING IS STILL THE SAME FOR A LOW TO BRING WIDESPREAD PRECIPITATION AS EARLY AS WED AFTERNOON AND CONTINUE DURING THE OVERNIGHT OF CHRISTMAS EVE. FRONTAL PASSAGE TIMING IS STILL SOMETIME AFTER MIDNIGHT. THEN CONTINUED SHOWERS AND PRECIPITATION ON CHRISTMAS MORNING WITH CLEARING DEVELOPING IN WESTERN ZONES ON CHRISTMAS AFTERNOON. TEMPERATURES DURING THIS EVENT ARE TRENDING COLDER AND NOW WHAT WAS ONCE A SLOPPY RAIN-SNOW EVENT FOR LOWER ELEVATIONS IS APPEARING TO BE ALL SNOW. IF THE COLDER TRENDS CONTINUE...THE SNOW TO WATER CONTENT RATIOS COULD INCREASE...RESULTING IN MORE SNOW. CURRENTLY...IT APPEARS LIKE A WINTER WEATHER ADVISORY SNOW EVENT FOR ALL BUT SOME LOCATIONS SUCH AS THE IDAHO WASATCH/BEAR RIVER RANGE AND THE CARIBOU NATIONAL FOREST MOUNTAINS...AS WELL AS THE SAWTOOTH RANGE IN CENTRAL IDAHO. ALSO TETON PASS APPEARS TO GET QUITE A HIT. THESE AREAS CAN EXPECT ONE FOOT TO NEARLY TWO FEET DURING THE 30 HOUR PERIOD FROM WED NOON THROUGH CHRISTMAS AFTERNOON. THE COLD FRONT LATE WED NIGHT AND INTO CHRISTMAS MORNING WILL ALSO COME WITH BREEZY TO WINDY CONDITIONS...SO BLOWING AND DRIFTING SNOW WILL ADD TO THE HAZARDOUS WEATHER.  FOR THE OTHER DAYS IN THIS FORECAST PERIOD...DRY AND COLD WILL CONTINUE TODAY THROUGH WED MORNING. WIND LIKE LAST SUNDAY AND YESTERDAY WILL NOT RETURN.  MESSICK  .LONG TERM...FRIDAY THROUGH NEXT TUESDAY. THE GFS AND ECMWF WERE IN VERY GOOD AGREEMENT THIS MORNING SHOWING A WEAK TRAILING DISTURBANCE SPREADING ACROSS THE NRN ROCKIES FRIDAY HELPING TO SUSTAIN A CHANCE/SLIGHT CHANCE MENTION OF SNOW SHOWERS FAVORING THE MOUNTAINS. A BRIEF RESPITE FOLLOWS EARLY SATURDAY AS THE NEXT PAC STORM ADVANCES INTO THE NW COAST SATURDAY AFTERNOON. WIDESPREAD SNOW FOLLOWS SUNDAY/MONDAY AS THE STORM SPREADS SOUTH THROUGH SRN IDAHO FOLLOWED BY A DRY AND COLDER NNE POLAR FETCH TUESDAY JUST TO REMIND US ITS STILL WINTER. HUSTON &&  .AVIATION...A FEW LOWER STRATUS CLOUDS LINGERED OVER THE MOUNTAINS IN THE DRY NNW FLOW EARLY THIS MORNING...OTHERWISE VFR CONDITIONS WERE FAIRLY PREVALENT EAST OF THE RIDGE BUILDING ALONG THE COAST. NUMERICAL MODELS WERE SHOWING ELEVATED NEAR SURFACE HUMIDITY VALUES AT KBYI...KPIH AND KIDA THIS MORNING. HOWEVER...THE OBSERVED TEMP AND DEW POINT SPREAD WAS FAIRLY MODEST AT BOTH KPIH AND KIDA WHICH MAY HINDER FOG DEVELOPMENT AT THESE TERMINALS THIS MORNING. OUTSIDE OF ANY LOCAL MORNING FOG DEVELOPMENT...VFR CONDITIONS SHOULD PREVAIL THROUGH WEDNESDAY MORNING. HUSTON &&   .PIH WATCHES/WARNINGS/ADVISORIES...
