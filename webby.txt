itemprop="articleBody">       <div class="shazam wiki_markup "  id="content_7192977" >Well here is my review of the Dell E6400 Business Class Laptop as promised. Sorry about the delay, I just got busy.<br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Dell E6400 Review-by rock3ralex</span></b><br><br> The specs for the configuration that is being reviewed:<br><br> Screen: 14.1" WXGA+ 1440x900 Widescreen<br> Hard Drive: 7,200 RPM 80Gb Hitachi<br> Processor: T9400 @ 2.53ghz w/ 6mb L2 Cache<br> Memory: 4GB DDR2-800mhz PNY Dual Channel Ram<br> Video Card: Nvidia Quadro NVS 160m 256mb vRam<br> Optical Drive: DVD 8x burner<br> Battery: 6 cell 54Whr.<br> Wireless: Dell 1397 wireless ABG Card<br> ATT Cell Network Card<br> Sound/Speaker: Integrated<br> Extras: Free Fall Sensor and 2 megapixel webcam<br><br><span style="font-size:large;"><b>Layout and Impressions</b></span><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i31.tinypic.com/359wbpe.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> My initial impressions when i first received the laptop was that I saw a very subtle, yet somewhat stylish laptop. The casing of the laptop is a thin, yet very sturdy aluminum. The backside of the screen is a brushed aluminum and adds a bit of style to this business class notebook. The aluminum also adds a lot of sturdiness to the laptop. The shell doesn't flex at all and makes it feel like you got what you paid for. The aluminum is also nice because you don't pick up a lot of finger prints like you would on a glossy surface.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i26.tinypic.com/2cn6x6t.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> I opened up the laptop and found that I have to press a button to unlock the hinge of the screen from the lower body of the laptop. Some might find this to be a pain, but I personally like this because it makes the E6400 feel very sturdy. There is no flop of the screen against the lower body whatsoever. When the laptop is open, you a greeted with a nicely proportioned 14.1" screen. The bezel around the screen is aluminum, just like the rest of the laptop. Above the screen, there is a small 2 megapixel webcam. It's performance is very good. The clarity is what you would expect from webcams nowadays and there was very little if any motion blur when I moved around while the camera was on.<br><br><span style="font-size:medium;"><b>Ports</b></span><br><br> For an average user, this laptop has a lot of ports.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i28.tinypic.com/1581abt.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> The left side of the laptop has a VGA out port, one usb port and an eSata port for those who want to use a high speed external hard drive. Further down the left side is the vent where the air from the heat sink is expelled out of the shell. The air coming out is slightly above ambient.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i25.tinypic.com/t6dr0k.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> Two more USB ports can be found on the right side of the E6400. There is also a mic and headphone jack along with the wifi toggle switch. Something that I haven't seen before on a laptop is a button with what appears to be another wifi logo. What this button does is activates a little pop up in windows that shows all wireless networks that can be found. This seems very handy and keeps you from having to navigate through the wireless software that comes with the laptop. Your general 8x dvd burner is also here along with a firewire and PC Card slot.<br><br> The front of this computer houses an SD Card reader and the back has the power jack and a gigabit ethernet port.<br><br><span style="font-size:large;"><b>Size and Weight</b></span><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i30.tinypic.com/2hdp5js.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> The Dell e6400 is definitely not your less than an inch, lightweight ultra-portable...Though it is relatively thin and lightweight for it's size. The laptop weighs in at 5.1 pounds with the 6 cell battery. Most laptops in 13.3-14.1" class weigh between 3.5-4lbs. The extra pound added by the aluminum shell is worth it because it adds a lot of ruggedness to the laptop without having to get into the tough-book style class. I have no doubt that if I dropped the laptop, it wouldn't be severely as would a laptop with a plastic/ABS shell.<br><br> This is pretty thin for its weight. The laptop is 1.25" thick closed and only .875" thick at the base. Not to shabby for a laptop housing so many gizmos.<br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Keyboard and Trackpad/Trackball and speakers</span></b><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i31.tinypic.com/okr0py.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> Dell managed to squeeze a full size keyboard into this small package along with a dedicated navigation keys (ie home, end, page up). As with all laptop there are function keys in the F row above the numbers. They start with sleep mode, charge mode, battery mode, lock and video out.<br><br> The Aesthetic parts of the keyboard very good. I find it very easy to type on and rarely hit a key by mistake. The keys aren't spaced apart as seen on other notebooks but the keys on here are large enough to type on even with very large hands, that I myself have.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i30.tinypic.com/xe4thz.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> The trackpad is very smooth but not glossy. It's very easy to slide you finger across and move the mouse without any problems. The left and right click buttons has a nice touch to them. they're not too stiff or so weak that you think they might break. The track ball in between the G&amp;H keys is also nice. The buttons below the space bar have a nice touch to them too.<br><br> Dell includes some very useful software that allows you to adjust every aspect of the touchpad and touchball. You can adjust the double click sensitivity, reverse the click buttons for lefties, mouse movement and scrolling speed. I also find it very useful that you can adjust the touch sensitivity and scrolling bar widths to be very useful because of my large hands. Other nice features include the ability to disable both the pad and the ball. I have the ball always disabled so I don't hit it on accident while typing. I also have it setup to where the trackpad becomes disabled when i plug in an external mouse, making it very easy to type accurately. I am typing this entire review on this laptop!!!<img alt="" class="inlineimg" src="/images/smilies/wave2.gif" style="border:0px solid;" title="Wave2"><img alt="" class="inlineimg" src="/images/smilies/wave2.gif" style="border:0px solid;" title="Wave2"><br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Screen:</span></b><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i28.tinypic.com/ao5eo2.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> A 14.1" screen is the perfect combination of viewing size to weight for a lightweight laptop. It keeps the weight of the laptop down, an full pound less than the E6400s big brother. I opted for the high resolution WXGA+ screen. The extra pixels give you a bit more viewing room and make it easier to view full sized web pages. Photo editing is also much easier. The colors that appear on the screen are very true and all photos and videos look superb.<br><br> The screen has 17 brightness adjustments allowing you to pick just the right level for your environment. During the night, the second brightness level is perfect for being able to read without burning your eyes. Any level above 10 is great for the day, allowing you to see the screen without a problems in direct sunlight.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i27.tinypic.com/4fv1hl.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i32.tinypic.com/i6crvn.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> The viewing angles for this screen are actually quit good. Even with the laptop rotate 45 degrees or more, you are still able to see what's on the screen without much discoloration.<br><br><span style="font-size:large;"><b>Performance---Battery Life, boot times and snappiness</b></span><br><br> For a laptop with a dedicated video, the battery life is very good. With a 6 cell battery. I am able to get 4.5 hours + with wifi on while I'm just using Firefox. If I'm using other applications or doing photo editing I get about 3 hours. When the laptop is under full load, cpu and gpu, the battery life goes down to 1hr and 54 minutes.<br><br> The dedicated video card in this laptop is designed to be for businesses and designers. It's definitely not for gaming but games that are a year or more old can be played on lower settings. Video playback is very smooth and HD content is handled with ease. Rendering is also very smooth.<br><br> Using a stop watch, I measured the boot time of this laptop. I clocked it in from the time I pressed the power button to the time I was able to start using applications. The 7200 rpms drive booted into Vista Ultimate 64 bit in 55 seconds. Not too shabby for a laptop.<br><br> The overall snappiness of the laptop is very quick. I can have multiple web pages and application open and laptop doesn't really hang up thanks to the fast processor and 4 gb of ram.<br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Upgrades:</span></b><br><br><img alt="" src="http://i31.tinypic.com/20kdfgo.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> The E6400 was designed to be a very modular notebook. The Optical Drive can be removed by taking out one screw and pull on the spring loaded tab. The hard drive can also be removed by taking out two screws and pull on the SC frame. The internals of the laptop are just as easily accessed. Take out the two hard drive screws and loosen the spring loaded screw. Then just pull the bottom panel away from the battery and presto.<br><br><img alt="" src="http://i28.tinypic.com/309u6fb.jpg" style="border:0px solid;"><br><br> Access to the internals is as simple as it gets. No nightmares of taking apart the entire laptop and fear of voiding the warranty! Ram can be interchanged just pull pulling on the two side tabs and popping it out. The cpu can be accessed very easily along with the gpu. Thermal paste can be interchanged. There are also two blank spots to add any type of wireless card you could want and wires to hook up the antennas.<br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Connectivity:</span></b><br><br> You have you choice of either a Dell G or N card along with the same from Intel. I have the Dell Wireless G and the range on it is excellent. I am able to connect to my router from 150+ feet and go through 3-4 solid walls with no problems. Bluetooth is also available along with an built in 3G card from your choice of service provider. The software to control these cards is pretty easy to use.<br><br><b><span style="font-size:large;">Overall Impression and Conclusion:</span></b><br><br> I am extremely impressed with this laptop. It's build quality is supeb.The screen is beautiful and the keyboard and mouse are very easy to use. The laptop is very lightweight and can be taken with out anywhere. Battery life is very good. This laptop would be a very nice one for any college students. The 3 year warranty, solid construction, build configurations make this ideal:<br><br> I know that this will last me all the way through college and will hold up to the dorm life.<br><br> Here are my ratings for the laptop on a scale of 1/10<br><br> Build/Weight: 9.5/10 (Very solid though is a bit heavier than other laptops in it's class. This isn't a downturn, just keep it in mind)<br> Battery life/performance: 9/10 (Performance is fast and battery life is good but goes down due to the dedicated video card)<br> Aesthetic Appeal: 8/10 (Not fancy but is very nice to look at. Might seem like an older notebook from afar but is different up close)<br><br> Overall: 26.5/30 88.3% I would highly recommend this laptop to anyone, especially college students.<br><br> If you have any benchmarks or tests you want run, I can probably do them sometime in the next week.
