href="http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/Google-Officially-Announces-Google-Fiber-in-Austin-123804">bringing Google Fiber to  Austin</a>, the company stated they expected Austin users to start being hooked up around the middle of 2014. The halfway of the year point rolled on past, without any new hard deadline for a launch or even the "fiberhood" system they use to determine deployment neighborhoods. Aside from a <a href="http://googlefiberblog.blogspot.com/2014/09/pardon-our-dust-in-austin.html">September blog post</a> with a sort of "shucks, building big networks takes time" tone, Google had been mute on a new timeline.<br><br><!-- tiny_attach --><table align=left><tr><td><A HREF="/r0/download/2188340~dd63017e0a5da4194e76d67130a84e74/Gig.jpg"><IMG class="apic" id="p13280" BORDER=0 HEIGHT=102 WIDTH=200 SRC="/r0/download/2188340.thumb200~dd63017e0a5da4194e76d67130a84e74/Gig.jpg/thumb200.jpg" ALT="Click for full size"></A><br></table>Yesterday however during a <a href="http://www.bizjournals.com/austin/news/2014/10/15/google-fiber-sets-austin-rollout-for-december.html">media event held in Austin</a> Google announced that they'll begin signups for South and Southeast Austin starting in December. As with Kansas City and Provo, locations that show the most interest will be first in line to receive the service. <br><br>The company didn't specify pricing, but it's expected to be the <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/kansascity/plans/">same as Kansas City</a>: $70 for symmetrical 1 Gbps, or $120 for symmetrical 1 Gbps with TV. Google also confirmed they'll also offer 5 Mbps for free after a $300 installation fee (that fee is <a href="https://fiber.google.com/cities/provo/plans/">$30 in Provo</a> where fiber was already heavily deployed). <br><br>While Google said they'd start signups in December and offer a website with more details on the Austin build, it's unclear when the first users will actually see service. href="http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/ATT-Offers-70-1-Gbps-in-Austin-With-a-Big-Catch-126969">offering 1 Gbps service</a> for $70 to $100 to a small selection of high-end developments in the
