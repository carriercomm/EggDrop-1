may never be resolved, insists Dish boss Charlie Ergen. Customers <a href="http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/Dish-Customers-Lose-Access-to-CNN-Other-Turner-Networks-130958">lost access to Turner channels</a> including CNN several weeks ago, and as with other retrans fights, both sides have been busy using websites to try and direct consumer ire toward the <i>other guy</i> (See Turner's <a href="http://savemyshows.com/">SaveMyShows.com website</a> and Dish's <a href="http://www.dishstandsforyou.com/">DishStandsForYou.com website</a>). <br><br><!-- tiny_attach --><table align=left><tr><td><A HREF="/r0/download/2191393~6edcf20f1472913eb5f486af799c3055/Dish%20Dropped.png"><IMG class="apic" id="p13292" BORDER=0 HEIGHT=88 WIDTH=200 SRC="/r0/download/2191393.thumb200~6edcf20f1472913eb5f486af799c3055/Dish%20Dropped.png/thumb200.jpg" ALT="Click for full size"></A><br></table>To hear Ergen tell it, CNN isn't the darling it once was, and its absence <a href="http://deadline.com/2014/11/dish-networks-charlie-ergen-loss-cnn-turner-channels-nonevent-1201273061/">isn't going to be missed</a>, even on election night: <BLOCKQUOTE><SMALL>quote:</SMALL><HR>When we take something down we&#8217;re prepared to leave it down forever. Things like CNN are not quite the product that they used to be. You can imagine: CNN down on election night would have been a disaster 15 or 20 years ago. Now there are plenty of other places for people to get news. In fact a lot of people get news not from TV but from their devices.<HR></BLOCKQUOTE>Ergen also says that without those channels, the company won't have to raise prices next year: <BLOCKQUOTE><SMALL>quote:</SMALL><HR>If we&#8217;re not going to be in a relationship with Turner then we would not have to raise our prices next year. And that would be slightly cash positive for us from a cash flow perspective. Yes, we listen to customers. But we would save a big, big, big check from a cash flow perspective. And for those folks who don&#8217;t care about news and cartoons, we have other news and cartoon shows.<HR></BLOCKQUOTE>Which sounds great for Dish, but so far customers haven't seen refunds after paying the same amount of money for less content. And Ergen's bluffing to some degree -- the company clearly can't tell all of its broadcasting partners to go to hell. Dish's primary business is TV, and unlike <a href="http://www.dslreports.com/shownews/Programming-Costs-Have-Small-Cable-Operators-Giving-up-on-TV-130701">some of the smaller cable operators leaving
