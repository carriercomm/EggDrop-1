That makes it even smaller than the already-diminutive <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2014/11/25/2016-smart-forfour-quick-spin-review/">Smart Forfour</a> or <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2014/10/01/2014-renault-twingo-first-drive-review/">Renault Twingo</a>, but into that compact form, Honda has fit five doors and four seats under a tall roof. In fact those front seats even fold flat and flush with the rear ones to make for as big a bed as you could possibly fit inside its compact dimensions.<br/> <br/> Technical details remain scarce, but power likely comes from the same 600cc three-cylinder engine - turbocharged or not, driving the front wheels or all four - as the rest of the N series, which already includes the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/11/02/honda-drops-full-details-about-incredibly-adorable-n-one/">N-One</a>, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2011/12/01/honda-n-box-is-done-with-mirrors-w-video/">N-Box</a>, <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2012/07/09/hondas-versatile-little-n-box-makes-the-scene/">N-Box+</a> and N-WGN.
