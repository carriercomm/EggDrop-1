                             <p>After videos depicting child pornography were allegedly found on Matthew Greenstein's laptop computer, he admitted to installing file-sharing software to download the videos, authorities said.</p><p>But Greenstein, 42, of suburban Lake Worth, reportedly told investigators he spent nearly six months downloading the videos because he wanted to report them to police.</p><p>That explanation was not enough to keep Palm Beach County Sheriff's deputies from arresting Greenstein this week and charging him with possession of child pornography. He was booked into the Palm Beach County Jail on Tuesday and remained in custody Wednesday night in lieu of $10,000 bail.</p><p>According to a sheriff's arrest report, a deputy with the South Florida Internet Crimes against Children task force found the internet address to Greenstein's computer in September while searching a law-enforcement database. The database contained a log of computers suspected of sharing and downloading child pornography.</p><p>Last month, investigators obtained a subpoena and requested subscriber information from the internet service provider. The company confirmed that the internet protocol address was assigned to Greenstein, the report said.</p><p>On Monday, investigators obtained a search warrant and they went to Greenstein's home the following day.</p><p>While at the home, deputies conducted a preview of Greenstein's computer and found a video of a young girl engaged in sexual activity, the arrest report said.</p><p>___<br/>
