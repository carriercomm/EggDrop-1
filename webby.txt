title="Man Haron Monis">Man Haron Monis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-age-Monis_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age-Monis-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-abc-Monis_8-1" Wales">New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. He took employees and customers hostage.</p> <p>After several hours, five of the hostages escaped, while an unknown number remained inside the building. At 2:14&#160;a.m. on 16 December, shortly after the apparent escape of more hostages, <a href="/wiki/State_Protection_Group" title="State Protection Group">police tactical operators</a> stormed the café.<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-police-over-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and later confirmed that Haron died in the confrontation; two other people also died.<sup id="cite_ref-police-release_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-police-release-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Hostages had been seen holding up a black <a href="/wiki/Black_Standard#Jihadist_black_flag" Media outlets advised that the hostages were not released, but had escaped, though details were not immediately available.<sup id="cite_ref-LIVE_STREAM:_Gunman_forcing_hostages_to_stand_against_cafe_windows_in_Sydney_siege_6-2" class="reference"><a id="cite_ref-foxnews1215_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews1215-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>At around 4:58&#160;p.m., two female hostages, both employees, ran from another entrance of the building, having also escaped,<sup id="cite_ref-LIVE_STREAM:_Gunman_forcing_hostages_to_stand_against_cafe_windows_in_Sydney_siege_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gunman_and_Two_Others_Dead_in_Sydney_Hostage_Crisis-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends_as_commandos_storm_Lindt_Cafe_17-1" id="Raid_and_end_of_siege">Raid and end of siege</span></h3> <p>At 2:14&#160;a.m. on 16 December, heavily armed police stormed the café following a barrage of <a href="/wiki/Stun_grenade" title="Stun grenade">flash bang grenades</a>, after which further hostages ran from the building in two groups. The first emerged after the initial gunshots, while the second emerged after the following volley.<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-smh-127suz_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh-127suz-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Police declared the siege over soon after,<sup id="cite_ref-police-over_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-police-over-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> later confirming that Haron died in the raid.<sup id="cite_ref-police-release_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-police-release-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hostages">Hostages</span></h2> <p>It was initially estimated that there were 13 people inside. Subsequent to the debriefing of the first group of escaped hostages, authorities advised that no more than 10 hostages remained inside the café.<sup id="cite_ref-LIVE_STREAM:_Gunman_forcing_hostages_to_stand_against_cafe_windows_in_Sydney_siege_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LIVE_STREAM:_Gunman_forcing_hostages_to_stand_against_cafe_windows_in_Sydney_siege-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Following the end of the siege, a total of 17 hostages were confirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>On 16 December, two hostages were killed and three others were wounded during the raid on the cafe. The slain hostages were described as a 34-year-old man and a 38-year-old woman.<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> One of the wounded hostages, a woman in her forties, suffered a gunshot wound to the leg and was treated at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_North_Shore_Hospital" title="Royal North Shore Hospital">Royal North Shore Hospital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> One of the hostages who initially escaped the scene was hospitalised for a preexisting condition<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> and was in a satisfactory condition.<sup id="cite_ref-Gunman_.27with_a_political_motivation.27_holds_hostages_in_Sydney.2C_Australia_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gunman_.27with_a_political_motivation.27_holds_hostages_in_Sydney.2C_Australia-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perpetrator">Perpetrator</span></h2> <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Man_Haron_Monis" title="Man Haron Monis">Man Haron Monis</a></div> <p>Man Haron Monis, sometimes known as Mohammad Hassan Manteghi,<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> was identified as the hostage-taker in the siege. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, and sought and was granted political asylum in Australia in 1996 following the detention of his wife and children.<sup id="cite_ref-Profile:_Man_Haron_Monis_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Profile:_Man_Haron_Monis-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Police_Name_Sydney_Hostage-Taker_as_.27Radical_Cleric.27_Man_Haron_Monis_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Police_Name_Sydney_Hostage-Taker_as_.27Radical_Cleric.27_Man_Haron_Monis-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> He was a self-proclaimed <a href="/wiki/Sheikh" title="Sheikh">sheikh</a>, although he was not recognised as such in the Islamic community.<sup id="cite_ref-age-Monis_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age-Monis-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-abc-Monis_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-Monis-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Police have currently found no connections between Haron and international terrorist organisations.<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_hostage_crisis_ends.3B_gunman_dead_after_raid-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Haron had a history of legal problems. Between 2007 and 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_Hostage_Crisis_Over_After_Police_Storm_Cafe-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> he protested the presence of Australian troops in <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">Afghanistan</a>, by writing letters to the families of soldiers killed there, in which he called the soldiers murderers<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> and urged the soldiers' families to petition the government to remove its troops from Afghanistan; one of the letters compared a deceased soldier to a pig and called his body "contaminated".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> He also sent similar letters to the families of British soldiers and the mother<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> of a government official killed by a bombing in <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. Haron was arrested on charges of "using a postal or similar service to menace, harass or cause offence", all of which he pleaded guilty to in 2013. He was reportedly sentenced to probation and 300 hours of community service. He was also banned from using the <a href="/wiki/Postal_service_in_Australia" title="Postal service in Australia">Australian postal service</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_Hostage_Siege_Ends_With_Gunman_and_2_Captives_Dead_as_Police_Storm_Cafe_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_Hostage_Siege_Ends_With_Gunman_and_2_Captives_Dead_as_Police_Storm_Cafe-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Before_the_Sydney_siege.2C_alleged_gunman_Man_Haron_Monis_faced_sexual_assault.2C_murder_conspiracy_charges_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Before_the_Sydney_siege.2C_alleged_gunman_Man_Haron_Monis_faced_sexual_assault.2C_murder_conspiracy_charges-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>In November 2013, he was charged with being an accessory to the murder of his ex-wife, Noleen Hayson Pal, who was allegedly stabbed 18 times and set alight in a stairwell on 21 April of that year. His partner, Amirah Droudis, was formally charged with Pal's murder.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup><sup arrested and charged with sexually and indecently assaulting a young woman in western Sydney in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_Hostage_Siege_Ends_With_Gunman_and_2_Captives_Dead_as_Police_Storm_Cafe_40-2" class="reference"><a sexual assault charges relating to at least six incidents that occurred between 2000 and 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_siege_gunman_named_as_Man_Haron_Monis-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> At the time of the siege, Haron had been free on bail for both aforementioned cases.<sup </div> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span></h3> <p>Trains were not stopping at <a href="/wiki/Martin_Place_railway_station" title="Martin Place railway station">Martin Place railway station</a> due to the hostage situation. <a href="/wiki/Transport_New_South_Wales" title="Transport New South Wales">Transport New South Wales</a> advised people to stay away from the CBD.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> Road closures also occurred. There was no southbound access to the <a href="/wiki/Cahill_Expressway" title="Cahill Expressway">Cahill Expressway</a>, <a href="/wiki/York_Street,_Sydney" title="York Street, Sydney">York Street</a>, or Harbour Street. There was no northbound access to the Cahill Expressway, and all traffic was diverted to the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Harbour_Tunnel" title="Sydney Harbour Tunnel">Sydney Harbour Tunnel</a>. Northbound traffic on the Western Distributor was diverted over Sydney Harbour Bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> Various road closures are in place.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider-happening_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-happening-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>On the morning of 16 December, road diversions remained in place and Martin Place train station remained closed.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Evacuations_and_closures">Evacuations and closures</span></h3> <div class="thumb tright"> <div <p>Immediately after the siege began, people found above the café were evacuated by ladder.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Opera_House" title="Sydney Opera House">Sydney Opera House</a> was evacuated after a suspicious package was found, however reports were unconfirmed by police.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> The U.S. Consulate General in Sydney, located in Martin Place, was also evacuated.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Police advised people in the area bounded by <a href="/wiki/Hunter_Street,_Sydney" title="Hunter Street, Sydney">Hunter</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Street,_Sydney" title="King Street, Sydney">George</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Street,_Sydney" title="Elizabeth Street, Sydney">Elizabeth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Street,_Sydney" title="Macquarie Street, Sydney">Macquarie Streets</a>, bordering Martin Place, to remain indoors and away from windows.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Bank" title="Commonwealth Bank">Commonwealth Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westpac" title="Westpac">Westpac</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Australia_and_New_Zealand_Banking_Group" title="Australia and New Zealand Banking Group">Australia and New Zealand Banking Group</a> closed their CBD branches for the day.<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/State_Library_of_New_South_Wales" title="State Library of New South Wales">State Library</a> also closed its doors.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> Numerous other buildings, including <a href="/wiki/David_Jones_Limited" title="David Jones Limited">David Jones</a> stores, executive offices for the <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Parliament" title="New South Wales Parliament" class="mw-redirect">New South Wales Parliament</a>, criminal courts for the <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Supreme_Court" title="New South Wales Supreme Court" class="mw-redirect">Supreme Court</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Downing_Centre" title="Downing Centre">Downing Centre</a>, and "several city legal chambers" were evacuated,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> as were the facilities of the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Network" title="Seven Network">Seven Network</a>, situated directly across from the café.<sup id="cite_ref-7lockdown_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7lockdown-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Some Sydney schools were put in "white level lockout" due to the hostage crisis, which meant that no school group was permitted to leave the school grounds.<sup id="cite_ref-smhlindt1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smhlindt1-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_reactions">Domestic reactions</span></h2> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span></h3> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Abbott" title="Tony Abbott">Tony Abbott</a>, convened the National Security Committee of Cabinet to give briefings on the situation.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-smhlindt1_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smhlindt1-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Abbott made a public statement reassuring Australians, stating, "Australians should be reassured that our law enforcement and security agencies are well trained and equipped and are responding in a thorough and professional manner."<sup id="cite_ref-Hostages_Taken_at_Australian_Chocolate_Shop_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hostages_Taken_at_Australian_Chocolate_Shop-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> He later said, "The whole point of politically-motivated violence is to scare people out of being themselves. Australia is a peaceful, open, and generous society. Nothing should ever change that and that's why I would urge all Australians today to go about their business as usual."<sup id="cite_ref-Abbott_speaks_to_nation:_We_won.27t_let_them_scare_us_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abbott_speaks_to_nation:_We_won.27t_let_them_scare_us-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_New_South_Wales" title="Premier of New South Wales">Premier of New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Baird" title="Mike Baird">Mike Baird</a>, addressed the media, stating "We are being tested today... in Sydney. The police are being tested, the public is being tested, but whatever the test we will face it head on and we will remain a strong democratic, civil society. I have full confidence in the Police Commissioner and the incredible work of the NSW police force."<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span></h3> <p>Due to concerns about anti-Muslim backlash and attacks, a grassroots movement named <i>#illridewithyou</i> was started on social media in order to physically and emotionally support Muslims travelling alone on public transport.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Muslim_organisations">Muslim organisations</span></h3> <p>Sanier Dandan, the president of the <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_Muslim_Association" title="Lebanese Muslim Association">Lebanese Muslim Association</a>, informed <a href="/wiki/ABC_News_(Australia)" title="ABC News (Australia)">ABC News</a> that Australian Muslim leaders were meeting online for discussions on how the Muslim community could help with the crisis. He also added that it was not known if the perpetrator or perpetrators were linked to the Australian Muslim community, saying, "Regardless we have a hostage situation. Whether he is someone who belongs to the Australian Muslim community or not, we are still waiting for information to be provided by police and based on that if there's something the Muslim community can do or assist, we are there."<sup id="cite_ref-Sydney_hostage_crisis:_Live_Report_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sydney_hostage_crisis:_Live_Report-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider-live_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-live-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> Ibrahim Abu Mohamed, the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Mufti" title="Grand Mufti">Grand Mufti</a> of Australia, also condemned the incident in a released statement.<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Central_Sydney_Is_in_Lockdown_Amid_a_Developing_Hostage_Crisis_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Central_Sydney_Is_in_Lockdown_Amid_a_Developing_Hostage_Crisis-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup> During the afternoon of 15 December, around 50 Muslim groups issued a joint statement in which they condemned the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-abc-blog_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-blog-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span></h2> <ul> <li><a href="/wiki/Crime_in_Australia" title="Crime in Australia">Crime in Australia</a></li> <li><a href="/wiki/Terrorism_in_Australia" title="Terrorism in Australia">Terrorism in Australia</a></li> <li><a href="/wiki/2014_Australian_terror_raids" title="2014 Australian terror raids">2014 Australian terror raids</a></li> </ul> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span></h2> <div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
