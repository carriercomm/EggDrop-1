<td>Flight deck and enclosed hangar for one helicopter</td> </tr> </table> <p>The <b><i>Atago</i> class</b> of guided missile <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">あたご型護衛艦｜atago-gata-go-ei-kan</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an improved version of the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Maritime_Self-Defense_Force" title="Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force">Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force</a> (JMSDF)'s <a href="/wiki/Kong%C5%8D_class_destroyer" title="Kongō class destroyer" class="mw-redirect"><i>Kongō</i> class destroyers</a>.</p> <p>Both ships of the class share a same name with Japanese World War II cruisers. class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atago-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Ashigara%26Mt.Inasa%26Mitsubisi.JPG/220px-Ashigara%26Mt.Inasa%26Mitsubisi.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" class="thumbcaption"> <div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ashigara%26Mt.Inasa%26Mitsubisi.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> JDS Kongo with <i>Ashigara</i> while under construction at Mitsubishi in Nagasaki.</div> </div> </div> <p>In 2000, the Japan Defense Agency Maritime Staff Office included another two <a href="/wiki/Aegis_combat_system" title="Aegis combat system" class="mw-redirect">Aegis</a> ships in its five-year budget on top of the four <i>Kongo</i> class destroyers originally ordered.</p> <p>The <i>Atago</i> class is fundamentally an improved and scaled up version of <a href="/wiki/Kong%C5%8D_class_destroyer" title="Kongō class destroyer" class="mw-redirect"><i>Kongō</i> class destroyers</a>. It features large accommodation and is capable of flexible operation. One of the most obvious changes is an additional hangar to carry one <a href="/wiki/SH-60_Seahawk" title="SH-60 Seahawk" class="mw-redirect">SH-60K</a> helicopter. In comparison to the <i>Kongō</i>-class/<i>Arleigh Burke</i>-class (Flight I) which only had <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> platforms (but no support equipment), these ships have better <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> handling facilities. To enhance <i>Atago</i> class' function as command centers, the bridge is two floors higher than <i>Arleigh Burke</i>-class Flight IIA, making her full load displacement over 10,000 tons—the first time for a JMSDF surface combat vessel. The gun caliber has extended from the 54 caliber of the <i>Kongō</i> class to the 62 caliber with strengthened powder charge capable 38&#160;km firing range . As with other Japanese ships being refit, the American-made <a href="/wiki/Harpoon_missile" title="Harpoon missile" class="mw-redirect">Harpoon missiles</a> (such as in the initial configuration of the <i>Kongō</i> class) have been replaced with the Japanese-made Type 90 (<a href="/wiki/SSM-1B" title="SSM-1B" class="mw-redirect">SSM-1B</a>) surface-to-surface guided missiles.</p> <p>Japan has also purchased a manufacturing license for these weapons for use on their Kongo class Aegis destroyers. Japan Steel Works will manufacture, assemble and test the weapons.</p> <p>The fire-control system for the <i>Atago</i> is <a href="/wiki/Aegis_combat_system" title="Aegis combat system" class="mw-redirect">Aegis Weapon System Baseline 7 phase 1</a>, which will combine American- and Japanese-manufactured systems to make up the complete Aegis system. The Aegis Weapon System baseline 7 has improved tracking accuracy for vertical targets, and an acquisition capability for small low-altitude targets compared with the Aegis Weapon System baseline 4 and 5, used in <i>Kongō</i> class. The <i>Atago</i> also use a new stealthier plain-structure mast, which was originally designed in Japan, rather than familiar lattice type mast. New modified smokestack and other improvements are also introduced to make <i>Atago</i> stealthier.</p> <p>Like the <i>Kongō</i> class, the <i>Atago</i> destroyers are equipped with a comprehensive suite of weapon systems including:</p> <ul> <li>Japan Type 90 (<a href="/wiki/SSM-1B" title="SSM-1B" class="mw-redirect">SSM-1B</a>) <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missile</a></li> <li>96-cell Mk-41 <a href="/wiki/Vertical_Launch_System" title="Vertical Launch System" class="mw-redirect">VLS</a> (64 cells in the forward area, 32 cells in the stern area) <ul> <li><a href="/wiki/RIM-66_Standard" title="RIM-66 Standard">RIM-66 Standard</a> <a href="/wiki/Surface-to-air_missile" title="Surface-to-air missile">surface-to-air missile</a></li> <li><a href="/wiki/RUM-139_VL_ASROC" title="RUM-139 VL ASROC" class="mw-redirect">RUM-139 VL ASROC</a></li> </ul> </li> <li>Two Mark 15 20&#160;mm <a href="/wiki/CIWS" title="CIWS" class="mw-redirect">CIWS</a> gun mounts</li> <li>Two <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedo</a> mounts in a triple-tube configuration</li> <li>One Mk 45 Mod 4 127&#160;mm 62-caliber gun, in a stealth-shaped mount. Made by Japan Steel Works under an American license from its original manufacturer.</li> </ul> <p>In keeping with Japan's post-war pacifist <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Japan" title="Constitution of Japan">constitution</a>, the <i>Atago</i> class does not currently carry the <a href="/wiki/Tomahawk_missile" title="Tomahawk missile" class="mw-redirect">Tomahawk missile</a> (although, in theory at least, use of an anti-ship version is permissible). While the two ships of <i>Atago</i> class are entering service, the <a href="/wiki/Tachikaze_class_destroyer" title="Tachikaze class destroyer" class="mw-redirect">Tachikaze class destroyers</a>, <i>Tachikaze</i> and <i>Asakaze</i> are to be decommissioned.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ships_in_the_class">Ships in the class</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
