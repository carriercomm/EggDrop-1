data-diply="0">With news circulating around the topic of gender equality lately, this artist is taking the topic one step further through his series called "<a href="http://www.jjlevine.com/alone-time/ " data-diply="1" target="_blank">Alone Time</a>" and "<a href="http://www.jjlevine.com/switch/" data-diply="0" target="_blank">Switch</a>." Montreal-based artist <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/JJ-Levine-Photography/134995563184422 " data-diply="2" target="_blank">JJ Levine</a> has created two series that depict a couple in traditional, domestic situations. Whether it be at prom, in the kitchen, in the bedroom, or in the bathroom, the couple is seemingly composed of a man and a woman. However, after a few moments, the viewer will realize it is either the same model depicting both roles in the case of "Alone Time," or a switch in gender performance in "Switch." Levine explains this concept in stating, "By demonstrating an individual body’s capacity to engagingly and believably embody two genders, my project questions the mainstream depiction of binary gender roles."&nbsp;</p><p data-diply="0">Levine's work is provocative in that it asks viewers to think outside of dominant cultural norms.
