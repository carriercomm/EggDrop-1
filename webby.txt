class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayne_Static&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Wells was born in <a href="/wiki/Muskegon,_Michigan" title="Muskegon, Michigan">Muskegon, Michigan</a> to Richard and Darlene Wells<sup id="cite_ref-IMDb_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-foxy_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxy-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Deseret_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deseret-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Shelby_Township,_Michigan" title="Shelby Township, Michigan">Shelby, Michigan</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He was three years old when he received his first toy guitar. His parents decided at age seven to get him his first real guitar, an S12 beginner model. He was given lessons, which paid off a year later when he won a talent contest playing "<a href="/wiki/Skip_to_My_Lou" title="Skip to My Lou">Skip to My Lou</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He later played in his first band at the age of 12 and decided he wanted to be a musician. In high school, he played in a band for the dances with classmates.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Shelby_High_School_(Michigan)" title="Shelby High School (Michigan)">Shelby High School</a> and had summer jobs which included working as an asparagus picker and in a cherry canning factory.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayne_Static&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>After moving to Chicago, Wells created the band Deep Blue Dream in 1987 with <a href="/wiki/Ken_Jay" title="Ken Jay">Ken Jay</a>, bass player <a href="/wiki/Eric_Harris_(musician)" title="Eric Harris (musician)">Eric Harris</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Corgan" title="Billy Corgan">Billy Corgan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The group was short lived as they relocated to California and Static-X was formed along with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Campos" title="Tony Campos">Tony Campos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Fukuda" title="Koichi Fukuda">Koichi Fukuda</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Static was recognizable for his unusual hairstyle; his hair was held up in a vertical position, a process that took about 20 minutes to complete.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> He was known for his signature "chintail" beard.</p> <p>Static announced in July 2007 that he would begin a side project, Pighammer,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> but in a December 2007 interview with Ultimate-Guitar.com, Static stated: "The Pighammer thing will happen when I have time. At the moment though, Static-X will remain my main priority." Static appears on the cover of the 2009 Static-X release, <i><a href="/wiki/Cult_of_Static" title="Cult of Static">Cult of Static</a></i>.</p> <p>Static's solo album <i>Pighammer</i> was released on October 4, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> As part of his <i><a href="/wiki/Pighammer" title="Pighammer">Pighammer</a></i> solo-project,<sup id="cite_ref-Ultimate_Guitar_Interview_Excerpt_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ultimate_Guitar_Interview_Excerpt-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Static formed a new line-up consisting of <a href="/wiki/Brent_Ashley" title="Brent Ashley">Brent Ashley</a> on bass, Sean Davidson on drums, and Ashes on guitar. In 2012, this line-up went under the name Static-X for the Noise Revolution tour. However in October 2012 the remainder of the tour was cancelled due to Static requiring medical treatment.</p> <p>He has confirmed that Static-X had disbanded in June 2013. According to Static, the disputes between himself and the band's former bassist Tony Campos over the rights to the band name had gone too far, forcing him to disband the four-piece.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>He was set to play a co-headlining tour in the US with <a href="/wiki/Powerman_5000" title="Powerman 5000">Powerman 5000</a> in November with <a href="/wiki/American_Head_Charge" title="American Head Charge">American Head Charge</a> supporting them.<sup id="cite_ref-Co-headline_tour_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Co-headline_tour-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayne_Static&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Static was an <a href="/wiki/Atheist" title="Atheist" class="mw-redirect">atheist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vegetarian" title="Vegetarian" class="mw-redirect">vegetarian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> After a brief courtship,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> he married adult film actress <a href="/wiki/Tera_Wray" title="Tera Wray">Tera Wray</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> on January 10, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> They lived in <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Tree,_California" title="Joshua Tree, California">Joshua Tree, California</a>. Static appeared as a mystery guest on an adult film Wray hosted called <i>Radium Vol 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayne_Static&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Static died in his sleep on November 1, 2014, three days before his 49th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> His death was rumoured as being caused by a drug overdose; the family later issued a statement that his death was not drug-related and happened in his sleep. Wayne was cremated in California and memorial services are pending in California and Kentucky <sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a
