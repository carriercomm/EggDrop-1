href="/wiki/Storytelling_System" title="Storytelling System">Storytelling System</a></td> </tr> </table> <p><i><b>Vampire: The Masquerade</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">role-playing game</a> (RPG) created by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rein-Hagen" title="Mark Rein-Hagen">Mark Rein-Hagen</a> and released in 1991 by <a href="/wiki/White_Wolf_Publishing" title="White Wolf Publishing">White Wolf Publishing</a> as the first of several <a href="/wiki/Storytelling_System" title="Storytelling System">Storytelling System</a> games for its <a href="/wiki/World_of_Darkness" title="World of Darkness">World of Darkness</a> setting line.<sup id="cite_ref-HG_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HG-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>It is an RPG set in a fictionalized "gothic-punk" version of the modern world, where players assume the roles of <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampires</a>, who are referred to as "Kindred", and deal with their night-to-night struggles against their own bestial natures, vampire hunters and each other.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1992, <i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Origins_Award" title="Origins Award">Origins Award</a> for <i>Best Roleplaying Rules of 1991</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The game line was discontinued in 2004 followed by a revision of the setting in <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Requiem" title="Vampire: The Requiem">Vampire: The Requiem</a></i>.</p> <p>A 20th Anniversary Edition of <i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> was printed in 2011 and the game was officially revived as part of White Wolf Publishing's shift to a print on demand business model,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and multiple new Masquerade products have been announced.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> All of White Wolf's tabletop roleplaying games are now owned and published by <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Path_Publishing" title="Onyx Path Publishing">Onyx Path Publishing</a>, including <i>Vampire</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Several associated products were produced based on Vampire: the Masquerade, including dice, <a href="/wiki/Collectible_card_game" title="Collectible card game">collectible card games</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Eternal_Struggle" title="Vampire: The Eternal Struggle">Vampire: The Eternal Struggle</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_%E2%80%93_Bloodlines" title="Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines">Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_%E2%80%93_Redemption" title="Vampire: The Masquerade – Redemption">Vampire: The Masquerade – Redemption</a></i>), and numerous novels. In 1996, a short-lived but successful television show loosely based on the game, <i><a href="/wiki/Kindred:_The_Embraced" title="Kindred: The Embraced">Kindred: The Embraced</a></i>, was produced by <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox Broadcasting Company</a>.</p> <p></p> </div> <p></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>The game uses the cursed and immortal vampiric condition as a backdrop to explore themes of morality, depravity, the human condition (or appreciation of the human condition in its absence), salvation, and personal horror. The gloomy and exaggerated version of the real world that the vampires inhabit, called the "<a href="/wiki/World_of_Darkness" title="World of Darkness">World of Darkness</a>," forms an already bleak canvas against which the stories and struggles of characters are painted. The themes that the game seeks to address include retaining the character's sense of self, humanity, and sanity, as well as simply keeping from being crushed by the grim opposition of mortal and supernatural antagonists and, more poignantly, surviving the politics, treachery, and often violent ambitions of their own kind.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_system">Game system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Game system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p><i>Vampire</i> is based on the Storytelling System. In addition to the general Storytelling rules, it uses a number of specific mechanics aimed towards simulating the vampiric existence. A vampire has a <i>blood pool</i> signifying the amount of human blood or <i>vitae</i> currently in their body; this blood can be spent to power abilities and perform supernatural tricks. These tricks simulate many of those portrayed on film, such as turning into animals or mist, sleeping in the ground or having unnatural charisma and powers of hypnotic suggestion.</p> <p>Close to the central theme of the game is the Humanity mechanic. Each vampire has a Humanity score, measuring how closely in touch with his <a href="/wiki/Human_nature" title="Human nature">human nature</a> the vampire is; as it decreases, the vampire becomes more susceptible to his <i>Beast</i>, the feral side of the vampiric soul that is driven entirely by rage and hunger. Brutal, immoral actions risk lowering a vampire's Humanity score. If the individual's Humanity drops to zero, the Beast takes over and the vampire is in a state of constant frenzy known as <i>Wassail</i>.</p> <p>The actions taken during gameplay are expressed using <a href="/wiki/Pentagonal_trapezohedron" title="Pentagonal trapezohedron">ten-sided</a> <a href="/wiki/Dice" title="Dice">dice</a>. The number of dice used correspond to the player's current skill level, often based on two different skills that together represent the player's ability. For example, to land a punch, the character's dexterity and brawl skill are combined. The resulting number is the number of die rolled to perform the task. It is up to the story teller to set how high a dice roll must be to be considered a success (usually 6 for standard actions).</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" title="Masquerade society">Masquerade society</a></div> <p>In the World of Darkness, most vampires in the game refer to themselves and their race as "Kindred" although some vampires, specifically members of the Sabbat faction, refer to themselves as "Cainites" as the curse that transforms them into vampires is said to have originated from the blood of <a href="/wiki/Cain_and_Abel" title="Cain and Abel">Caine</a>. The term "<a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/kine" class="extiw" title="wikt:kine">Kine</a>", an archaic term for <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, refers to humans.</p> <p>Characters within the <i>Vampire</i> setting are members of one of the vampiric clans or minor bloodlines and usually belong to factions associated with these or that reflect a general ideological stance the characters happen to share. For example, a vampire of the Brujah bloodline might identify as a member of the Camarilla, Sabbat or Anarchs sects but very few Tremere vampires would be found among the Sabbat and even more rarely among the Anarchs. Some clans and most of the minor bloodlines declare themselves independent from any sects. A vampire who rejects all associations with any sect and clan is known as "Autarkis". Laibon, called <a href="/wiki/Kindred_of_the_Ebony_Kingdom" title="Kindred of the Ebony Kingdom">Kindred of the Ebony Kingdom</a> by Western Kindred, are not so much a sect as a cultural group bound together loosely by a powerful spiritual bond to the land and the people of Africa.</p> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Kindred_of_the_East" title="Kindred of the East">Kindred of the East</a>, while sharing some superficial similarity to the western Kindred, are actually an entirely different variety of supernatural being.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_13_Clans">The 13 Clans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The 13 Clans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p><i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> introduces the use of 13 clans (or major bloodlines) in the game:</p> <ul> <li><i>Assamite</i>: A clan of Middle Eastern assassins. Their Discipline of Quietus allows them to move soundlessly and gives them power over the blood of other vampires.</li> <li><i>Ventrue</i>: A clan of aristocrats and bureaucrats. Most princes come from this clan. Their Disciplines of Dominate and Fortitude allows them to control the minds of mortals and vampires alike and grants them supernatural resilience respectively.</li> <li><i>Followers of Set</i>: A clan of cultists who worship their Antediluvian progenitor, the Egyptian snake god <a href="/wiki/Set_(mythology)" title="Set (mythology)">Set</a>.</li> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Malkavian" title="Malkavian">Malkavian</a></i>: A clan of lunatics whose madness grants them strange insight. Their Discipline of Dementation allows them to spread their insanity like a plague.</li> <li><i>Ravnos</i>: A clan of gypsies and charlatans who hail from India. Their Discipline of Chimerstry allows them to alter perceptions to create illusions.</li> <li><i>Nosferatu</i>: A clan of deformed vampires who often dwell in the shadows of the sewers. Their Discipline of Obfuscate allow them to hide in the shadows.</li> <li><i>Lasombra</i>: A clan with the power to control shadows. Their Discipline of Obtenebration allows them to command darkness and shadows.</li> <li><i>Giovanni</i>: A clan of mostly Italian merchants and gangsters, who Embrace within their own human family. Their Disciplines specialize in Necromancy. They were originally a bloodline of the former Clan Cappadocian, who they have since usurped.</li> <li><i>Brujah</i>: A clan of hot-headed malcontents, rebels, rogues and anti-authoritarians who strive for freedom from societal restraints. Their Disciplines of Potence and Presence grants them superhuman strength and supernatural charisma respectively.</li> <li><i>Toreador</i>: A clan of sensualists and artists. Their Discipline of Celerity gives them superhuman speed.</li> <li><i>Gangrel</i>: A clan of animalistic vampires highly in-tune with their Beasts. Their Disciplines of Animalism and Protean allow communion with animals and the power of shape-shifting respectively.</li> <li><i>Tzimisce</i>: A clan of scholars and flesh-shapers. Their Discipline of Vicissitude allows them to reshape the flesh and bone of themselves and other creatures.</li> <li><i>Tremere</i>: A clan of blood sorcerers. They were originally a bloodline of Clan Tzimisce until their founder, a mage named Tremere, diablerized the Antediluvian of the former Clan Salubri, Saulot, solidifying the Tremere's status as a clan. Their Discipline of Thaumaturgy allow them to use the power of their blood to cast spells.</li> </ul> <p>Each Clan can trace its origins to one of 13 elder vampires known as an <a href="/wiki/Antediluvians_(World_of_Darkness)" title="Antediluvians (World of Darkness)">Antediluvian</a>, for they survived <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>'s biblical flood. Each Antediluvian is a “grandchilde” of <a href="/wiki/Cain" title="Cain" class="mw-redirect">Caine</a>, who killed <a href="/wiki/Abel" title="Abel" class="mw-redirect">Abel</a> and was cursed by God into becoming the first vampire. Through the back story of the game, Antediluvians started a war among themselves, called the <a href="/wiki/Jyhad" title="Jyhad" class="mw-redirect">Jyhad</a>, and use their clansmen to fight this war for them.</p> <p>Bloodlines, on the other hand, either cannot trace their lineage to an Antediluvian founder or are too little in number to be considered a major player in the <a href="/wiki/Jyhad" title="Jyhad" class="mw-redirect">Jyhad</a>. Some Bloodlines are considered to be off-shoots of existing clans. All Bloodlines are treated as exceptionally rare in the game, leaving most of the interactions and story lines centered around the Clans.</p> <p>Each Clan and Bloodline has a unique set of powers called Disciplines, and their own set of weaknesses, also unique to that particular branch of vampire.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Factions">Factions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Factions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p><i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> offers the players the opportunity to play in a politically structured world in which sects (large factions) rule over all of vampire society. While many factions and sub-sects exist in the game, the main focus is the conflict between the Camarilla, the Sabbat and the Anarchs.</p> <p>The primary sect is the <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_society#Camarilla" title="Masquerade society">Camarilla</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Camarilla" title="Camarilla">camarilla</a>), sometimes called "<a href="/wiki/Ivory_tower" title="Ivory tower">the Ivory Tower</a>". They originated in the first attempt for control over the entire vampire population. Through their use of the Six Traditions, Camarilla Kindred survive the night, hiding their existence from humans.</p> <p>The second most populous sect is the <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_society#Sabbat" title="Masquerade society">Sabbat</a>. The Sabbat, sometimes called "the Sword of Caine", is the archenemy of the Camarilla; their goal is rule over humanity, which they see as their birthright.</p> <p>The third major sect is the <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_society#Anarchs" title="Masquerade society">Anarchs</a>, who oppose the rule of the Elders and wish to manage their own lives.</p> <p>Other minor sects include <a href="/wiki/Black_Hand_(World_of_Darkness)" title="Black Hand (World of Darkness)">The Black Hand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masquerade_society#Inconnu" title="Masquerade society">the Inconnu</a>. Independents are those who take no sides with any of the factions but have their own goals and agendas.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tie-ins_and_adaptations">Tie-ins and adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tie-ins and adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <ul> <li>Under the title <i>Mind's Eye Theatre: The Masquerade</i> White Wolf also provides a <a href="/wiki/Live_action_role-playing_game" title="Live action role-playing game">live action role-playing game</a> in the same setting, using their <i><a href="/wiki/Mind%27s_Eye_Theatre" title="Mind's Eye Theatre">Mind's Eye Theatre</a></i> system.</li> <li><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Eternal_Struggle" title="Vampire: The Eternal Struggle">Vampire: The Eternal Struggle</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Collectible_card_game" title="Collectible card game">collectible card game</a> based on "Vampire", was produced by <a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_the_Coast" title="Wizards of the Coast">Wizards of the Coast</a> and later by White Wolf.</li> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Kindred:_The_Embraced" title="Kindred: The Embraced">Kindred: The Embraced</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> series based on <i>Vampire</i>, was produced by <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a>.</li> <li>A <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> based upon the <i>Vampire</i> milieu is <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_-_Redemption" title="Vampire: The Masquerade - Redemption" class="mw-redirect">Vampire: The Masquerade - Redemption</a></i>, developed by <a href="/wiki/Nihilistic_Software" title="Nihilistic Software" class="mw-redirect">Nihilistic Software</a> and published in 2000 by <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>.</li> <li>Another game followed in 2004: <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_-_Bloodlines" title="Vampire: The Masquerade - Bloodlines" class="mw-redirect">Vampire: The Masquerade - Bloodlines</a></i>. Developed by <a href="/wiki/Troika_Games" title="Troika Games">Troika Games</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>, it uses <i><a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve</a></i>'s <a href="/wiki/Source_engine" title="Source engine" class="mw-redirect">Source engine</a>.</li> <li>A <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a>, called <i><a href="/wiki/Music_from_the_Succubus_Club" title="Music from the Succubus Club">Music from the Succubus Club</a></i>, was released by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.dancing-ferret.com/">Dancing Ferret Discs</a> to serve as a <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> for the <i>Vampire</i> <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">RPG</a>.</li> <li><a href="/wiki/Moonstone_Books" title="Moonstone Books">Moonstone Books</a> published a series of comic book adaptations of <i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> which are now hard to find, but some of them made it into <a href="/w/index.php?title=DriveThruRPG&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DriveThruRPG (page does not exist)">DriveThruRPG</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Print_on_Demand" title="Print on Demand" class="mw-redirect">Print on Demand</a> service.<sup id="cite_ref-DriveThru_Moonstone_WoD_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DriveThru_Moonstone_WoD-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></li> <li><a href="/wiki/Steve_Jackson_Games" title="Steve Jackson Games">Steve Jackson Games</a> published an adaptation of <i>Vampire: The Masquerade</i> using their popular <a href="/wiki/GURPS" title="GURPS">GURPS</a> generic table-top roleplaying system. They followed this book up with a supplement called <i>GURPS: Vampire Companion</i>. Both books were produced for use with the Third Edition of the GURPS rules and are no longer in print. The Steve Jackson company also produced GURPS conversions of <i>Werewolf: The Apocalypse</i>, and <i>Mage: The Ascension</i>. Steve Jackson Games had the original copyright to the World of Darkness setting. Initially, SJ Games decided not to release their version; however, after the massive popularity the game setting received during the White Wolf Company's release, SJ Games, which still held their official rights of release on the system, published its version.</li> </ul> <p><i><a href="/wiki/Werewolf:_The_Apocalypse" title="Werewolf: The Apocalypse">Werewolf: The Apocalypse</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mage:_The_Ascension" title="Mage: The Ascension">Mage: The Ascension</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wraith:_The_Oblivion" title="Wraith: The Oblivion">Wraith: The Oblivion</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Changeling:_The_Dreaming" title="Changeling: The Dreaming">Changeling: The Dreaming</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hunter:_The_Reckoning" title="Hunter: The Reckoning">Hunter: The Reckoning</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mummy:_The_Resurrection" title="Mummy: The Resurrection">Mummy: The Resurrection</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kindred_of_the_East" title="Kindred of the East">Kindred of the East</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Demon:_The_Fallen" title="Demon: The Fallen">Demon: The Fallen</a></i> are other <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">RPG</a> titles set in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_of_Darkness" title="Old World of Darkness" class="mw-redirect">Old World of Darkness</a>.</p> <p>In August 2004, the now-defunct game set in the original World of Darkness was replaced by <i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Requiem" title="Vampire: The Requiem">Vampire: The Requiem</a></i>. Although it is an entirely new game, rather than a continuation of the old, it uses many elements of the old game, including certain clans and disciplines.</p> <p>At the White Wolf <a href="/wiki/Camarilla" title="Camarilla">Camarilla</a> meeting in October 2009 it was decided to re-support this game system both in the official Camarilla fan club and outside to Table Top players. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.white-wolf.com">[1]</a></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_Anniversary_Edition">20th Anniversary Edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 20th Anniversary Edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>On March 17, 2011, White Wolf announced the 20th Anniversary Edition, which was published during the Grand Masquerade event in New Orleans on September 15–17, 2011, released to the attendees. Customers not attending The Grand Masquerade were offered a limited time preorder option. The 20th Anniversary Edition contains revisions of rules and is a compendium of most information provided in supplemental material in the game's earlier life.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Print_on_Demand">Print on Demand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Print on Demand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>As of fall 2011 White Wolf and online role playing game store DriveThruRPG.com began offering the new and classic World of Darkness source books in a print on demand format through the DriveThruRPG web site starting with a number of formerly out of print Vampire: The Masquerade books and gradually adding more as they were ready for print. DriveThruRPG and White Wolf have indicated that eventually all World of Darkness material will be available in this way.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <ul> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Eternal_Struggle" title="Vampire: The Eternal Struggle">Vampire: The Eternal Struggle</a></i></li> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_%E2%80%93_Bloodlines" title="Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines">Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines</a></i></li> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Vampire:_The_Masquerade_%E2%80%93_Redemption" title="Vampire: The Masquerade – Redemption">Vampire: The Masquerade – Redemption</a></i></li> <li><i><a href="/wiki/Kindred:_The_Embraced" title="Kindred: The Embraced">Kindred: The Embraced</a></i></li> </ul> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vampire:_The_Masquerade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;"> <ol class="references"> <li id="cite_note-HG-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-HG_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><span class="citation book"><a href="/wiki/George_Vasilakos" title="George Vasilakos">Vasilakos, George</a> (2007).
