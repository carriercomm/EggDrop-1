<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Westminster_School_Arch.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div> Westminster School</div> </div> </div> <p>Theroux is the son of the American travel writer and novelist <a href="/wiki/Paul_Theroux" title="Paul Theroux">Paul Theroux</a> (who is of French Canadian<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and Italian descent).<sup id="cite_ref-Ask_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ask-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> His older brother is the writer and television presenter <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Theroux" title="Marcel Theroux">Marcel Theroux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sons_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sons-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He is the cousin of American actor and screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Justin_Theroux" title="Justin Theroux">Justin Theroux</a>. Born in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, he moved to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> when he was one and was brought up in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Theroux was educated for a couple of years at Allfarthing Primary School then moved to <a href="/wiki/Westminster_School" title="Westminster School">Westminster School</a> (where he was a friend and contemporary of the comedians <a href="/wiki/Adam_Buxton" title="Adam Buxton">Adam Buxton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Cornish_(filmmaker)" title="Joe Cornish (filmmaker)" class="mw-redirect">Joe Cornish</a>). Another of his contemporaries was <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats" title="Liberal Democrats">Liberal Democrat</a> politician and current Deputy Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Clegg" title="Nick Clegg">Nick Clegg</a>, with whom he travelled to America.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He then went to <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_College,_Oxford" title="Magdalen College, Oxford">Magdalen College, Oxford</a>, where he gained a first class degree in history and was noted for his film reviews for the <i>Grapevine</i> magazine.</p> <p>His first employment as a journalist was with <i><a href="/wiki/Metro_Silicon_Valley" title="Metro Silicon Valley">Metro Silicon Valley</a></i>, an alternative free weekly newspaper in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a>. In 1992, he was hired as a writer for <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_(magazine)" title="Spy (magazine)">Spy</a></i> magazine. He was also working as a correspondent on <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moore" title="Michael Moore">Michael Moore</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Nation" title="TV Nation">TV Nation</a></i> series, for which he provided segments on off-beat cultural subjects, including selling <a href="/wiki/Avon_Products" title="Avon Products">Avon</a> to women in the <i><a href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" title="Amazon Rainforest" class="mw-redirect">Amazon</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem_syndrome" title="Jerusalem syndrome">Jerusalem syndrome</a> and the attempts by the <a href="/wiki/Ku_Klux_Klan" title="Ku Klux Klan">Ku Klux Klan</a> to rebrand itself as a civil rights group for white people. When <i>TV Nation</i> ended he was signed to a development deal by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, out of which came <i><a href="/wiki/Louis_Theroux%27s_Weird_Weekends" title="Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends">Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends</a></i>. He has guest-written for a number of publications including <i><a href="/wiki/Hip-Hop_Connection" title="Hip-Hop Connection" class="mw-redirect">Hip-Hop Connection</a></i> and he continues to write for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Idler_(1993)" title="The Idler (1993)">The Idler</a></i>.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentaries">Documentaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Documentaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <div class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Louis_Theroux_documentaries" title="List of Louis Theroux documentaries">List of Louis Theroux documentaries</a></div> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Louis_Theroux.27s_The_Ultra_Zionists"><i>Louis Theroux's The Ultra Zionists</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Louis Theroux&#039;s The Ultra Zionists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>Louis Theroux spends time with a small and very committed subculture of ultra-nationalist Jewish settlers. He discovers a group of people who consider it their religious and political obligation to populate some of the most sensitive and disputed areas of the West Bank, especially those with a spiritual significance dating back to the Bible.</p> <p>Throughout his journey, Louis gets close to the people most involved with driving the extreme end of the Jewish settler movement</p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Louis_Theroux.27s_Weird_Weekends"><i>Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Louis Theroux&#039;s Weird Weekends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Louis_Theroux%27s_Weird_Weekends" title="Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends">Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends</a></div> <p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Louis_Theroux%27s_Weird_Weekends" title="Louis Theroux's Weird Weekends">Weird Weekends</a></i> (1998–2000), Theroux followed marginal, mostly American subcultures like <a href="/wiki/Survivalism" title="Survivalism">survivalists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_nationalism" title="Black nationalism">black nationalists</a>, <a href="/wiki/White_supremacy" title="White supremacy">white supremacists</a> and porn stars, often by living among or close to the people involved. Often, his documentary method subtly exposed the contradictions or farcical elements of some seriously held beliefs. Theroux himself describes the aim of the series as: "Setting out to discover the genuinely odd in the most ordinary setting. To me, it's almost a privilege to be welcomed into these communities and to shine a light on them and, maybe, through my enthusiasm, to get people to reveal more of themselves than they may have intended. The show is laughing at me, adrift in their world, as much as at them. I don't have to play up that stuff. I'm not a matinee idol disguised as a nerd." Despite calls for a complete DVD set of the <i>Weird Weekends</i> series, only selected episodes have ever been available for purchase.</p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="When_Louis_Met..."><i>When Louis Met...</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: When Louis Met...">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/When_Louis_Met..." title="When Louis Met...">When Louis Met...</a></div> <p>In <i><a href="/wiki/When_Louis_Met..." title="When Louis Met...">When Louis Met...</a></i> (2000–02), Theroux accompanied a different British celebrity in each programme as they go about their day-to-day business, interviewing them about their lives and experiences as he did so. His episode about British entertainer <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Savile" title="Jimmy Savile">Jimmy Savile</a>, <i>When Louis Met Jimmy</i>, was voted one of the top fifty documentaries of all time in a survey by Britain's <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>. In <i>When Louis Met the Hamiltons</i>, the disgraced <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Tory</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament_(United_Kingdom)" title="Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect">MP</a> <a href="/wiki/Neil_Hamilton_(politician)" title="Neil Hamilton (politician)">Neil Hamilton</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Christine_Hamilton" title="Christine Hamilton">Christine</a> were arrested following false allegations of indecent assault during the course of filming.</p> <p>In <i>When Louis Met Max Clifford</i>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Clifford" title="Max Clifford">Max Clifford</a> tried to set Louis up. However, it backfired when Clifford was caught lying, as the crew was still recording his live microphone during a conversation. After this series concluded, a retrospective was aired called <i>Life with Louis</i>. He was meant to do a similar programme with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> before <a href="/wiki/Martin_Bashir" title="Martin Bashir">Martin Bashir</a> completed his documentary for ITV, but it was cancelled.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Theroux went on to make a documentary called <i><a href="/wiki/Louis,_Martin_%26_Michael" title="Louis, Martin &amp; Michael">Louis, Martin &amp; Michael</a></i> about his quest to get an interview with Michael Jackson. Selected episodes of <i>When Louis Met...</i> were included as bonus content on a Best-Of collection of <i>Weird Weekends</i>. The entire series has never been released on DVD.</p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BBC_Two_specials">BBC Two specials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: BBC Two specials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Louis_Theroux%27s_BBC_Two_specials" title="Louis Theroux's BBC Two specials">Louis Theroux's BBC Two specials</a></div> <p>In these special programmes, beginning in 2003, Theroux returned to American themes, working at feature-length, this time with a more natural tone. In March 2006, he signed a new deal with the BBC to make 10 films over the course of three years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Subjects for the specials include criminal gangs in Lagos, <a href="/wiki/Louis_and_the_Nazis" title="Louis and the Nazis">Neo-Nazis in America</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Ultra_Zionists" title="The Ultra Zionists">ultra-Zionists in Israel</a>, child psychiatry and the prison system in California and Florida. A 2007 special, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Most_Hated_Family_in_America" title="The Most Hated Family in America">The Most Hated Family in America</a></i>, received strong critical praise from the international media.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>His first book, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Call_of_the_Weird:_Travels_in_American_Subcultures" title="The Call of the Weird: Travels in American Subcultures" class="mw-redirect">The Call of the Weird: Travels in American Subcultures</a></i>, was published in Britain in 2005. In the book Theroux returns to America to find out what has happened in the lives of some of the people he featured in his television programmes since he last saw them.<sup id="cite_ref-sons_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sons-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Filmography">Filmography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_Theroux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Filmography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>As part of the <i>Weird Weekends</i> episode <b>Porn</b>, he agreed to a cameo in the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Gay_pornography" title="Gay pornography">gay pornography</a> release <i>Take a Peak</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> He did not perform sexual acts in the film, but made a brief appearance as a ranger in search of a criminal.</p> <p>In the <i>Weird Weekends</i> episode on <b>Infomercials</b> he was featured as a live-salesman for an at-home paper shredder for the <a href="/wiki/Home_Shopping_Network" title="Home Shopping Network">Home Shopping Network</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sons-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> He now
