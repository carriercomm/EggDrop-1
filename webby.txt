                 </div>                 <div class="multi_page_links">                                   </div>               </div>                         <div class="post_content">               <p align="left"><a href="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/nexus2cee_DSC5614-21.jpg"></a></p> <p align="left">Tap-to-wake is one of the niftier features Google's implemented on the <a title="Nexus 9" href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00M6UC5B4?tag=androidpolice-20&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;pf_rd_m=ATVPDKIKX0DER&amp;pf_rd_s=desktop-auto-sparkle&amp;pf_rd_r=163CXGKQCFSXRQDM4WN1&amp;pf_rd_p=1951326862&amp;pf_rd_t=301&amp;pf_rd_i=nexus+9&amp;qid=1413502150" class="external" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Nexus 9</a>, but its telephony-enabled sibling didn't get the same treatment. We know that it almost did, though, thanks to a commit to the device's source code <a href="https://android.googlesource.com/device/moto/shamu/+/2084f7d0cf50baf394759d175bc28ad80cf7cace" target="_blank" class="external" rel="nofollow">made on September 10th</a>. (We also pointed this out <a href="http://www.androidpolice.com/2014/11/12/nexus-6-review/#lollipop-on-the-nexus-6" target="_blank">in our review</a> of the Nexus 6).</p> <p align="center"><a href="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/nexus2cee_DSC57891.jpg"><img title="nexus2cee_DSC5789" style="display: inline" alt="nexus2cee_DSC5789" src="http://cdn.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/nexus2cee_DSC5789_thumb.jpg" width="668" height="304"></a></p> <p align="left">Google hasn't commented on this other than to make clear that tap-to-wake is not a feature of the Nexus 6. They also haven't asterisked that with any kind of conditional language like "currently" or "at this time," so it seems probable that it isn't coming back, either. Why was it removed in the first place? Again, we don't know - but it's an issue ripe for speculation.</p> <p align="left">If you ask me, it's exceedingly likely ambient display was deemed to have made tap-to-wake functionally redundant, because it kind of does.
