He is best known for portraying eccentric characters in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_in_Pink" title="Pretty in Pink">Pretty in Pink</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/Less_Than_Zero_(film)" title="Less Than Zero (film)">Less Than Zero</a></i> (1987), <i><a href="/wiki/Sex,_Lies,_and_Videotape" title="Sex, Lies, and Videotape">Sex, Lies, and Videotape</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Stargate_(film)" title="Stargate (film)">Stargate</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(1996_film)" title="Crash (1996 film)">Crash</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Secretary_(film)" title="Secretary (film)" class="mw-redirect">Secretary</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pentagon_Papers_(film)" title="The Pentagon Papers (film)">The Pentagon Papers</a></i> (2003), and <i><a href="/wiki/Lincoln_(2012_film)" title="Lincoln (2012 film)">Lincoln</a></i> (2012). His most famous television roles are those of attorney <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shore" title="Alan Shore" class="mw-redirect">Alan Shore</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Practice" title="The Practice">The Practice</a></i> and its spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i> (for which he won three <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Awards</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Robert_California" title="Robert California">Robert California</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)">The Office</a></i>. He currently stars as Raymond "Red" Reddington in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blacklist_(TV_series)" title="The Blacklist (TV series)">The Blacklist</a></i> and is set to play <a href="/wiki/Ultron" title="Ultron">Ultron</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a>' upcoming 2015 film <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Age_of_Ultron" title="Avengers: Age of Ultron">Avengers: Age of Ultron</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p></p> <div id="toc" class="toc"> <div id="toctitle"> class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Spader&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Spader was born in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect">Boston, Massachusetts</a>, the son of Jean (née Fraser) and Stoddard Greenwood "Todd" Spader, both teachers.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> During his early education, Spader attended <a href="/wiki/The_Pike_School" title="The Pike School">The Pike School</a> (where his mother taught Art) and enrolled in the <a href="/wiki/Brooks_School" title="Brooks School">Brooks School</a> (where his father taught) in <a href="/wiki/North_Andover,_Massachusetts" title="North Andover, Massachusetts">North Andover, Massachusetts</a> for one year. He later transferred to <a href="/wiki/Phillips_Academy" title="Phillips Academy">Phillips Academy</a> but dropped out in the 11th grade to pursue acting at the <a href="/wiki/Michael_Chekhov" title="Michael Chekhov">Michael Chekhov</a> School in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tvguide-bio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-bio-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Before becoming a full-time actor, he held a variety of jobs, including yoga instructor, busboy, truck driver, stable boy, and railroad car loader.<sup id="cite_ref-tvguide-bio_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-bio-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Spader&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Spader's first major film role was in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Endless_Love_(1981_film)" title="Endless Love (1981 film)">Endless Love</a></i> (1981), and his first starring role was in <i><a href="/wiki/Tuff_Turf" title="Tuff Turf">Tuff Turf</a></i> (1985). However, he did not rise to stardom until 1986, when he played the rich, arrogant playboy Steff in <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_in_Pink" title="Pretty in Pink">Pretty in Pink</a></i>. He co-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Mannequin_(1987_film)" title="Mannequin (1987 film)">Mannequin</a></i> (1987) and the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Less_Than_Zero_(film)" title="Less Than Zero (film)">Less Than Zero</a></i> (1987), in which he played a drug dealer named Rip. Supporting roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Baby_Boom_(film)" title="Baby Boom (film)">Baby Boom</a></i> (1987) and <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_(1987_film)" title="Wall Street (1987 film)">Wall Street</a></i> (1987) followed until his breakthrough in <i><a href="/wiki/Sex,_Lies,_and_Videotape" title="Sex, Lies, and Videotape">Sex, Lies, and Videotape</a></i> (1989), in which he played a sexual <a href="/wiki/Voyeur" title="Voyeur" class="mw-redirect">voyeur</a> who complicates the lives of three <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">Baton Rouge, Louisiana</a> residents. For this performance, he received the Best Actor Award at the <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-imdb-awards_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-awards-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Spader's roles in the early 1990s included a young, affluent widower opposite <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a> in the romantic drama <i><a href="/wiki/White_Palace_(film)" title="White Palace (film)">White Palace</a></i> (1990), <a href="/wiki/John_Cusack" title="John Cusack">John Cusack</a>'s best friend in <i><a href="/wiki/True_Colors_(1991_film)" title="True Colors (1991 film)">True Colors</a></i> (1991), and a poker-playing drifter in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Music_of_Chance_(film)" title="The Music of Chance (film)">The Music of Chance</a></i> (1993). In 1994, he starred as <a href="/wiki/Egyptology" title="Egyptology">Egyptologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Jackson_(Stargate)" title="Daniel Jackson (Stargate)">Daniel Jackson</a> in the sci-fi film <i><a href="/wiki/Stargate_(film)" title="Stargate (film)">Stargate</a></i>. In 1996, he played car accident fetishist James Ballard in the controversial Canadian film <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(1996_film)" title="Crash (1996 film)">Crash</a></i> and assassin Lee Woods in <i><a href="/wiki/2_Days_in_the_Valley" title="2 Days in the Valley">2 Days in the Valley</a></i>. In 1997, Spader guest starred in the <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Apology_(Seinfeld)" title="The Apology (Seinfeld)">The Apology</a>", as an angry recovering alcoholic who refuses to apologize to <a href="/wiki/George_Costanza" title="George Costanza">George</a> for making fun of him. In 2000, he played a drug-addicted detective tracking down a <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Watcher_(film)" title="The Watcher (film)">The Watcher</a></i>. In 2002, he starred as a <a href="/wiki/Sadomasochism" title="Sadomasochism">sadistic</a> boss in the critically acclaimed film <i><a href="/wiki/Secretary_(film)" title="Secretary (film)" class="mw-redirect">Secretary</a></i>.</p> <p>From 2004 to 2008, Spader starred as <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shore" title="Alan Shore" class="mw-redirect">Alan Shore</a> in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>, in which he reprised his role from the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Practice" title="The Practice">The Practice</a></i>. He won the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series">Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series</a> in 2004 for his portrayal on <i>The Practice</i> and won it again in 2005 and 2007 for <i>Boston Legal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-imdb-awards_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-awards-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> With the 2005 win, he became one of only a few actors to win an Emmy award while playing the same character in two different series. Even rarer, he won a second consecutive Emmy while playing the same character in two different series. He also won the <a href="/wiki/Satellite_Award" title="Satellite Award">Satellite Award</a> for Best Actor in a Series, Comedy or Musical for <i>Boston Legal</i> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-imdb-awards_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-awards-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>In October 2006, Spader narrated "<a href="/wiki/Discovery_Atlas:_China_Revealed" title="Discovery Atlas: China Revealed" class="mw-redirect">China Revealed</a>", the first episode of <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>'s documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/Discovery_Atlas" title="Discovery Atlas">Discovery Atlas</a></i>. He has also done the <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-over</a> in several television commercials for <a href="/wiki/Acura" title="Acura">Acura</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> He starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Race_(Mamet_play)" title="Race (Mamet play)" class="mw-redirect">Race</a></i>, a play written and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a>, which opened on December 6, 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Barrymore_Theatre" title="Ethel Barrymore Theatre">Ethel Barrymore Theatre</a> on Broadway.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The show closed on August 21, 2010 after 297 performances.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> In March 2011, he was named to star in the film <i><a href="/wiki/By_Virtue_Fall" title="By Virtue Fall" class="mw-redirect">By Virtue Fall</a></i>, written and to be directed by <a href="/wiki/Sheldon_Turner" title="Sheldon Turner">Sheldon Turner</a>. As of June 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=James_Spader&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the movie was in pre-production.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>Spader guest starred as <a href="/wiki/Robert_California" title="Robert California">Robert California</a> in "<a href="/wiki/Search_Committee" title="Search Committee">Search Committee</a>", the <a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._season_7)" title="The Office (U.S. season 7)">season 7</a> finale of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)">The Office</a></i>. On June 27, 2011, it was announced that he would join the cast on a permanent basis.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> He planned to stay only through the eighth season, and while the original plan was just to do the guest appearance, executive producer Paul Lieberstein said: "those two scenes became a season".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> <p>On August 29, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> announced that Spader would join the <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Age_of_Ultron" title="Avengers: Age of Ultron">Avengers: Age of Ultron</a></i> (2015) cast as villainous robot <a href="/wiki/Ultron" title="Ultron">Ultron</a>. Spader currently stars in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blacklist_(TV_series)" title="The Blacklist (TV series)">The Blacklist</a></i>, which premiered on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> September 23, 2013. He portrays Raymond "Red" Reddington, one of the <a href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI" class="mw-redirect">FBI</a>'s most wanted fugitives.</p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Spader&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Spader met his first wife, decorator Victoria Kheel, while working in a <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">yoga</a> studio after he moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in the 1980s. They married in 1987 and have two sons, Elijah and Sebastian. Spader filed for divorce from Kheel in 2004.
